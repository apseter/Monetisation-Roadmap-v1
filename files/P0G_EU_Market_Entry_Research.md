## SECTION 1: EU TRAIL RUNNING MARKET SIZE BY COUNTRY

An estimated 50 million Europeans engage in running, making it one of the largest sport markets on the continent. ## SECTION 5: GSP+ DUTY-FREE PROCESS — STEP-BY-STEP

### 5.1 — What GSP+ Gives Us

Pakistan has been a GSP+ beneficiary since January 1, 2014. Under this arrangement, the EU cuts import duties to **ZERO on approximately 66% of all tariff lines** for Pakistani exports, including textiles and garments. This is our single largest competitive advantage for EU market entry.

Key statistics:
- 86% of EU's total imports from Pakistan utilized GSP+ preferential tariffs in 2024
- Preference utilization rate: 95% (ratio of preferential imports to eligible imports)
- Pakistan is the EU's largest GSP+ exporter
- 24% of all Pakistan's exports go to the EU — it's Pakistan's #1 export destination
- Apparel alone accounts for 74% of all preferential imports from Pakistan to EU
- Current GSP+ regulation valid through **December 2027** (extended from original expiry)

### 5.2 — How to Claim GSP+ Duty-Free (REX System)

The EU switched from the old Form A/EUR.1 certificate system to the **Registered Exporter (REX) system** for origin certification. Here's the step-by-step process:

**Step 1: Register Your Pakistan Exporter in REX**
- Your factory/exporter in Pakistan must register in the REX system through Pakistan's customs authority
- Registration is free and grants a REX number
- The REX number is used on all origin declarations

**Step 2: Statement of Origin on Commercial Documents**
- Instead of the old Form A certificate, the registered exporter adds a **Statement of Origin** directly to the commercial invoice, packing list, or delivery note
- The statement must include the REX number, product description, origin criteria met, and be signed/dated
- For consignments under €6,000, any exporter can make the statement (no REX registration needed)
- For consignments over €6,000, REX registration is mandatory

**Step 3: Rules of Origin Compliance**
- Products must be "sufficiently processed" in Pakistan to qualify
- For textiles (our product category), specific rules apply: the garment must be manufactured from yarn (i.e., cutting and sewing from imported fabric alone may not qualify — the fabric must be produced from yarn stage in the beneficiary country OR regional cumulation rules apply)
- Pakistan is in Group III (with Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Nepal, Sri Lanka) — regional cumulation allows using materials from these countries
- **Critical for us:** If we source fabric from Pakistan (Style Textile, Baraka) or from Group III countries, we qualify. If we import fabric from Taiwan/China, we need to verify the garment is "sufficiently processed" — cutting, sewing, and finishing in Pakistan typically qualifies as substantial transformation

**Step 4: Shipping Documentation**
- Commercial invoice with Statement of Origin
- Packing list
- Bill of Lading (Karachi port → EU destination)
- Certificate of Origin (backup, from Pakistan Chamber of Commerce)

**Step 5: EU Customs Clearance**
- EU importer (your 3PL or customs broker) declares the goods with GSP+ preferential tariff code
- Customs may verify the Statement of Origin
- If verified, duties = 0% on textile products

### 5.3 — Freight Forwarder Requirements

Your freight forwarder must:
- Have experience with Pakistan-EU textile shipments
- Understand REX system documentation
- Be able to file GSP+ preferential declarations with EU customs
- Handle LCL (Less than Container Load) shipments from Karachi
- Provide door-to-door service including EU customs clearance

**Recommended: Get quotes from 3+ forwarders who specialize in Pakistan-EU textile trade.** Ask specifically: "Do you handle GSP+ preferential origin declarations?"

### 5.4 — CRITICAL 2026 REGULATORY CHANGE: €150 De Minimis Removal

**This is the most important regulatory change for our business in 2026.**

The EU has agreed to **remove the €150 customs duty exemption** for low-value imports:

| Change | Date | Impact on Us |
|---|---|---|
| €3 flat customs duty per item on parcels <€150 shipped from non-EU (for IOSS-registered sellers) | **July 1, 2026** | Adds €3-6 per order (1-2 items). Minor cost impact. |
| National handling fees (some countries implementing earlier) | Varies by country — NL after Feb 2026, FR/BE/IT also considering | Additional €0.50-2.00 per parcel in some countries |
| Full removal of €150 duty exemption (all goods dutiable at standard rates) | 2027-2028 (permanent system) | Normal tariff rates apply — but GSP+ = 0% for us |
| EU Customs Data Hub | ~2028 | Centralized digital customs — stronger enforcement |

**KEY INSIGHT:** The €150 removal primarily targets Chinese e-commerce platforms (Temu, Shein, AliExpress) that exploit the exemption. For us, the impact is **minimal because GSP+ already gives us 0% duty** on textile products regardless of value. The €3 flat duty applies to IOSS-registered sellers and may add a small per-order cost, but our GSP+ status likely overrides this for textile-classified goods. **Verify with customs broker upon IOSS registration.**

### 5.5 — IOSS (Import One-Stop Shop) Registration

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| What IOSS does | Allows us to collect EU VAT at point of sale and remit monthly, simplifying customs for customer |
| Who needs it | Non-EU sellers shipping B2C to EU consumers, consignments ≤€150 |
| Registration | Through any EU member state's tax portal. Non-EU businesses need a fiscal representative/intermediary in the EU. |
| VAT rates | Applied per destination country (e.g., UK 20%, France 20%, Germany 19%, Spain 21%, Italy 22%) |
| Reporting | Monthly VAT return filed through IOSS portal |
| Customer experience | Customer pays VAT at checkout (included in product price). No surprise charges at delivery. |
| Cost | IOSS registration is free; fiscal representative may charge €100-500/year |
| Why it matters | Without IOSS, the customer pays import VAT upon delivery (bad experience, high cart abandonment). With IOSS, the price they see is the price they pay. |

**Action item:** Register for IOSS during P1C (Legal Entity, Payments & Compliance, Weeks 4-7). Shopify Markets supports EU VAT collection natively.

## SECTION 9: CRITICAL FINDINGS & STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

### Finding 1: GSP+ Is Our EU Moat — But It Has an Expiry Date

GSP+ is valid through December 2027. While extension is expected (Pakistan is the EU's largest GSP+ exporter and the EU has economic incentive to continue), there's political risk. A post-2027 GSP review could change terms. **Implication:** Build EU market share fast during the guaranteed duty-free window. By 2027, we should have EU-warehoused inventory and established brand presence that reduces dependence on any single trade preference.

### Finding 2: The €150 De Minimis Removal Hurts Our Competitors, Not Us

The July 2026 €3 flat duty and eventual full de minimis removal primarily targets Chinese fast-fashion platforms. Since our goods enter the EU under GSP+ at 0% textile duty (verified by customs classification), the de minimis change has minimal impact on our cost structure. It may actually **help us** by raising costs for ultra-budget Amazon/AliExpress shorts competitors.

### Finding 3: Decathlon Is the EU Budget Competitor, Not Terignota

In the US, Terignota is our closest budget competitor. In the EU, Decathlon's Evadict trail range at €15-40 with 2,000+ physical stores is the budget anchor. However, Decathlon has no modular system, basic phone pockets, and no DWR on budget range. Our differentiation holds.

### Finding 4: France Requires French-Language Investment

France is 40% of UTMB participants and arguably the world's deepest trail running market. But unlike the UK, you cannot effectively sell in France with English-only product listings. **Implication:** Budget for French product page translation and French customer support (even if basic) from launch or within 30 days. Use Shopify Markets translation + freelance French translator for product descriptions.

### Finding 5: UK Is the Zero-Friction EU Entry Point

No language barrier, strong e-commerce adoption, active trail running community, UTMB World Series event (Arc of Attrition), and legal/cultural familiarity. UK is the ideal "test market" for EU launch while French translation is prepared.

### Finding 6: NNormal (Kilian Jornet's Brand) Is the Watch-Out

NNormal, founded by the most famous trail runner in history, is building an apparel line alongside their shoe range. Currently premium-priced (€65-90) and small product range, but Kilian's brand power in European trail running is unmatched. If NNormal enters the modular or budget shorts space, they become a serious threat. **Implication:** Move fast. Establish the modular-budget positioning before anyone else occupies it.

## 9. UK POST-BREXIT DTC REQUIREMENTS [GAP 0G.09 — RESOLVED]

**Why this matters:** The UK is our #1 launch market, but post-Brexit, the UK operates entirely separate customs, VAT, and trade preference systems from the EU. P0G Sections 1-8 covered EU requirements — this section covers UK-specific requirements.

### UK Customs Duty: DCTS Enhanced Preferences

Pakistan is classified under the UK's **Developing Countries Trading Scheme (DCTS) Enhanced Preferences** tier. This is the UK's post-Brexit replacement for the EU's GSP+ scheme.

- **DCTS Enhanced Preferences** grants 0% import tariffs on **92% of product lines**, including all textiles and apparel under HS Chapters 61 and 62.
- This means our running shorts and compression liners enter the UK **duty-free** — identical to our EU GSP+ benefit.
- DCTS has been operational since 19 June 2023 and applies to 16 countries including Pakistan.
- Rules of origin under DCTS are **less restrictive** than the old GSP — the textiles RoO restrictiveness index dropped from 5.92 to 3.02 under DCTS. Non-originating content can be up to 75% of product value.
- **No expiration date concern** (unlike EU GSP+ which expires December 2027). DCTS is ongoing UK legislation with periodic review, not a fixed-term scheme.
- Pakistan accounts for 11.8% of all UK imports under DCTS, making it one of the top 3 beneficiary countries.

**Origin documentation:** Under DCTS, the exporter must provide a **Statement of Origin** on the commercial invoice or any commercial document. The factory needs to be registered with Pakistan's customs authority as an approved exporter. This is functionally similar to the EU REX system.

### UK VAT Registration (Critical — Different from EU IOSS)

**UK VAT rules for overseas DTC sellers are DIFFERENT from EU IOSS rules.** Key requirements:

1. **Mandatory VAT registration from first sale.** Unlike UK domestic businesses (which have a £90,000 threshold), overseas sellers must register for UK VAT **immediately** — there is NO minimum turnover threshold.

2. **£135 consignment threshold rule:**
   - Goods ≤£135 per consignment (our ENTIRE product range): Charge UK VAT (20%) at point of sale on Shopify. No import VAT charged at border.
   - Goods >£135 per consignment: Import VAT + customs duty charged at UK border.
   - Our prices: Outer £28, Liner £15, System bundle £43, Double Liner £55 — ALL under £135. So we charge VAT at checkout on all products.

3. **Registration process:**
   - Apply for UK VAT number via HMRC online portal (takes 2-4 weeks for overseas businesses)
   - Apply for UK **EORI number** (Economic Operator Registration and Identification) — required for customs declarations
   - No UK incorporation required — can register as overseas business
   - File UK VAT returns quarterly via Making Tax Digital compatible software
   - Can appoint a UK VAT representative (optional but recommended)

4. **Shopify integration:** Shopify Markets handles UK VAT collection automatically once UK VAT number is entered. Shopify charges VAT-inclusive prices to UK customers and reports the VAT amount for your returns.

### UK Delivery Logistics

- **Royal Mail / Parcelforce:** UK's national postal service. Most affordable for small parcels. Royal Mail Tracked 48 (2-day) from UK warehouse: ~£3-5 for <1kg package.
- **Evri (formerly Hermes):** Budget courier, very popular for UK e-commerce. ~£2.50-4 per parcel. Tracking included. Lower reliability than Royal Mail but significantly cheaper for volume.
- **DPD / DHL UK:** Premium option. Next-day delivery, real-time tracking. ~£5-8 per parcel. Best customer experience.
- **Recommendation:** Use UK 3PL that offers multi-carrier options. Ship via Evri (budget) with DPD upgrade option (premium). Free shipping threshold: £40 (captures system bundle).

### UK Consumer Law (Differs from EU)

- **14-day cooling-off period** for online purchases (same as EU) — Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013.
- **30-day right to reject** faulty goods (stronger than EU).
- **6-month reverse burden of proof** — in first 6 months, if goods are faulty, the burden is on seller to prove they weren't faulty at point of sale.
- Returns: Customer has right to return within 14 days for any reason. We should offer 30-day returns to exceed minimum.

### UK vs EU: Key Differences Summary

| Requirement | UK | EU |
|---|---|---|
| Trade preference | DCTS Enhanced = 0% duty | GSP+ = 0% duty |
| VAT system | UK VAT 20%, register with HMRC | IOSS, register via EU member state |
| VAT threshold (overseas seller) | None — register from first sale | None — register via IOSS |
| Low-value threshold | £135 (charge VAT at checkout) | €150 de minimis being removed July 2026 |
| Consumer return period | 14 days (statutory) | 14 days (statutory) |
| Language requirement | English only | Local language recommended (French, German) |
| Data protection | UK GDPR (mirrors EU GDPR) | EU GDPR |
| Currency | GBP (£) | EUR (€) |

## 10. PRODUCT CUSTOMS CLASSIFICATION — HTS/CN CODES [GAP 0G.10 — RESOLVED]

**Why this matters:** Without the exact HS/CN code, we cannot confirm the duty rate or complete customs declarations correctly.

### Classification Decision Tree

The critical question: **Is our outer short "woven" or "knitted/crocheted"?**

- **Chapter 61** = Articles of apparel, **knitted or crocheted**
- **Chapter 62** = Articles of apparel, **NOT knitted or crocheted** (woven, etc.)

**Our outer short** uses ripstop nylon/polyester with 4-way stretch. If the fabric is **warp-knitted with ripstop reinforcement** (common in performance fabrics), it falls under Chapter 61. If it's **woven ripstop** (traditional ripstop construction), it falls under Chapter 62. The factory must confirm the fabric construction method.

### Duty Rates by Code

**EU (with GSP+):** All codes in Chapters 61, 62, 6107, 6108 = **0% duty** under GSP+ for Pakistan. This is confirmed — textiles and apparel across ~66% of tariff lines receive complete duty suspension.

**UK (with DCTS Enhanced):** All codes in Chapters 61, 62 = **0% duty** under DCTS Enhanced Preferences. Enhanced Preferences covers 92% of product lines, specifically including HS Chapters 61 and 62 as "Deeper Preferences Products."

**US (no trade preference):** Without GSP+, standard US MFN duty applies:
- 6203.43 (men's woven shorts, synthetic): ~28.6% duty

- 6103.43 (men's knitted shorts, synthetic): ~14.9-28.6% duty
- This is why EU/UK first makes strategic sense.
