## P2A.1: FABRIC SOURCING STRATEGY

### P2A.1.1: Pakistan Cannot Supply Performance Fabrics — Import from Shaoxing

Pakistan's textile industry processes $15+ billion in exports annually, but it is overwhelmingly cotton-centric. Every major mill assessed — Sapphire Fibres, Nishat Mills, Masood Textile, Crescent Textile — produces cotton or poly-cotton goods, not nylon-based performance stretch fabrics. The industry reality is stark: apart from basic polyester, synthetic performance fibres including nylon, viscose, and elastane blends are virtually all imported.

Interloop Limited (Faisalabad) is the sole Pakistani manufacturer with genuine nylon/spandex compression knitting capability using Italian machinery, supplying Nike and Adidas. However, they are a $476M revenue enterprise operating at massive scale and will not sell raw fabric to a startup at any quantity. They are functionally inaccessible for our purposes.

## P2A.2: SNAP ATTACHMENT MECHANISM ENGINEERING

### P2A.2.1: Snap Type Selection

**S-Spring ring snaps (#633, 12.5mm cap diameter) are the optimal choice.** They deliver 2-3 kg pull force per snap. With 6 attachment points, total hold reaches 12-18 kg — far exceeding what trail running demands while remaining easy to detach intentionally with one hand.

**Important competitive intelligence:** Path Projects, the industry leader in modular running shorts, uses NO snap attachment at all — their outer short and liner operate as fully independent garments with separate waistbands. For a first product entering the market, snap attachment creates a tangible, marketable connection point that customers can feel and understand. This is a differentiation advantage.

### P2A.2.2: Snap Specification

| Attribute | Specification |
|---|---|
| Type | S-Spring ring snap, 4-part (cap + socket + stud + post) |
| Size | #633 (12.5mm cap diameter) |
| Material | Brass with nickel-free finish (skin contact compliance) |
| Pull force | 2-3 kg per snap (12-18 kg total with 6 points) |
| Durability target | 500+ attach/detach cycles before force degradation |
| Certification required | Oeko-Tex 100, ASTM D7142 (90N fabric pull-out test) |

### P2A.2.3: Snap Placement Engineering

**6 snap points along the inner waistband ONLY:**

1. **2 at center front** — flanking the drawcord exit, 3cm apart
2. **2 at side seams** — positioned over hip bones
3. **2 at center back** — flanking the phone pocket, 3cm apart

**Mount the socket (recessed, smoother) on the LINER and the stud on the OUTER SHORT.** This ensures the stud (slightly raised) faces away from skin when the liner is worn without the outer.

**DO NOT place attachment points below the waistband.** Lower points restrict independent movement and increase chafe risk.

### P2A.2.4: What to Avoid

| Mechanism | Why NOT for trail running shorts |
|---|---|
| Magnetic snaps | Poor shear resistance — will accidentally detach during dynamic running movements |
| Hook-and-loop (Velcro) | Collects lint, degrades with washing, irritates skin |
| Cam snaps | Too stiff for comfortable athletic use, difficult one-handed operation |
| Buttons | Slow, can press into skin under vest/belt pressure |

## P2A.3: FLATLOCK SEAM CAPABILITY ASSESSMENT

### P2A.3.1: Where Flatlock Is Essential vs Not

This distinction saves significant complexity and cost:

| Component | Seam Location | Required Stitch Type | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Outer short | Side seams, inseam | 4-thread overlock | Not against skin; overlock is industry standard |
| Outer short | Hem | Coverstitch or topstitch | Clean finish, standard equipment |
| Outer short | Waistband | Lockstitch + overlock | Structural, standard equipment |
| **Liner** | **ALL body seams** | **FLATLOCK (critical)** | **Against skin in high-friction zones. Non-negotiable.** |
| Liner | Leg opening | Coverstitch or flatlock | Comfort at thigh |
| Liner | Waistband | Overlock + coverstitch | Under outer short, less critical |

### P2A.3.2: Family Factory Current Equipment (Typical Lahore Garment Factory)

Your family factory likely has:
- Single-needle lockstitch machines (Juki DDL-8700 or equivalent)
- 3/4-thread overlock machines (Juki MO-6814 or equivalent)
- Straight knife fabric cutter
- Steam iron / pressing equipment
- Basic cutting table

**What's MISSING for athletic apparel:**
- Flatlock machine (Yamato VF2500 or Pegasus W600)
- Coverstitch machine (for hems)
- Snap press machine
- Heat press (for reflective tape application)

### P2A.3.3: Flatlock Machine Options

| Machine | New Price | Used/Refurb Price | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Yamato VF2500 | $5,000-8,000 | $2,000-3,000 | Brandreth Road dealers, Tomsewing.com | "Active Thread Control" reduces operator skill barrier by 30-40% |
| Pegasus W600 | $4,500-7,000 | $1,800-2,500 | Brandreth Road, Liya International | Industry workhorse |
| Chinese clone (Siruba, Jack) | $1,500-2,500 | $800-1,500 | Alibaba, Brandreth Road | Lower reliability but adequate for 300-unit runs |

### P2A.3.4: The Skill Reality

**Flatlock on 4-way stretch nylon/spandex is the highest-skill sewing operation in a garment factory.** Training an experienced overlock operator to achieve production-quality flatlock on stretch fabric takes **6-12 weeks** of dedicated practice.

Common defects during the learning curve:
- Seam popping under stretch
- Puckering and waviness
- Oil staining from machine
- Inconsistent stitch density
- Thread breakage on stretch recovery

**Thread specifications:** Ball-point (SUK) needles size 70/10, core-spun polyester needle thread (Tex 27-40), texturized polyester in the loopers for skin comfort and stretch recovery.

## P2A.4: PHASED HYBRID MANUFACTURING STRATEGY

## P2A.5: TECH PACK CREATION & PATTERN DEVELOPMENT

### P2A.5.1: Hire a Freelance Activewear Tech Pack Designer

**Do not attempt tech packs without athletic apparel expertise.** Hire on Upwork or Fiverr for **$300-600** for both the outer short and liner tech packs.

**Also purchase 2-3 reference garments:** Patagonia Strider Pro, Nike Dri-FIT Stride, Path Projects Graves PX. These communicate design intent to the factory far more effectively than any document. Budget **$100-200** for competitor products.

### P2A.5.2: Tech Pack Contents Required

Each tech pack must include:

1. **Flat sketches** — front, back, inside views, exploded assembly view
2. **Complete BOM** — every component with specifications
3. **Measurement spec sheet** with tolerances per size
4. **Grading rules** — how measurements change XS through 3XL
5. **Construction callouts** — stitch types, SPI (stitches per inch) at each seam
6. **Colorway specifications** — Pantone references for each color
7. **Snap placement diagram** — exact measurements from reference points
8. **Packaging details** — polybag, labels, hang tags, fold method
9. **Assembly document** (unique to modular system) — how the two pieces connect

### P2A.5.3: Critical Measurements for Spec Sheet

**Outer Short:**
- Waist relaxed (half), waist stretched (full)
- Hip width (1" below waist seam)
- Front rise, back rise
- Inseam, outseam
- Leg opening
- Thigh width (1" below crotch)
- Waistband height
- Phone pocket: width, depth, opening width, zipper length

**Compression Liner:**
- Waist relaxed (half), waist stretched (full)
- Hip width
- Front rise, back rise
- Inseam
- Leg opening relaxed, leg opening stretched
- Gripper band width
- Snap placement positions (distance from center front, distance from waistband top)

### P2A.5.4: Tolerances

| Measurement Type | Tolerance |
|---|---|
| Critical circumferences (waist, hip) | ±½" (13mm) |
| Lengths (inseam, rise) | ±¼" (6mm) |
| **Snap placement** | **±⅛" (3mm) MAXIMUM** — misalignment prevents proper attachment |
| Leg opening | ±¼" (6mm) |

Tolerance must be less than half the grade increment between sizes.

### P2A.5.5: Pattern Development

**Hire a Lahore-based freelance pattern maker:** PKR 50,000-120,000 ($180-432) for both pieces across both genders.

**Critical:** Women's patterns MUST be developed independently. Different hip:waist ratio (0.70-0.80 vs men's 0.85-0.95), higher back rise, and different crotch curve make scaled-down men's patterns unacceptable.

The compression liner requires **12-18% horizontal negative ease** for 15-20 mmHg compression, calibrated to the exact production fabric. This means the liner pattern is physically smaller than the body measurement — the stretch of the fabric creates the compression.

### P2A.5.6: Size Simplification for First Run

**Strongly recommended: Reduce to 4-5 sizes (S through XL or 2XL) for the first run.** Full XS-3XL grading generates 36+ patterns across 2 genders x 2 components. Eliminating XS and 3XL (which together account for only ~5% of sales) cuts pattern work by 30%.

## P2A.6: SAMPLE DEVELOPMENT PROCESS

### P2A.6.1: Expected Sampling Rounds

For a complex modular product, expect **5-7 rounds:**

| Round | Purpose | Duration | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Proto sample 1 | Basic shape, proportions, pocket placement | 1-2 weeks | $30-60 |
| Proto sample 2 | Revised shape, snap mechanism testing | 1-2 weeks | $30-60 |
| Fit sample 1 | On-body fit testing, movement assessment | 1-2 weeks | $40-80 |
| Fit sample 2 | Revised fit based on wear-test feedback | 1-2 weeks | $40-80 |
| Size set | One sample per size to verify grading | 2-3 weeks | $150-300 |
| Pre-production sample | Final production-representative unit | 1 week | $40-80 |
| Top-of-production (TOP) | First 5-10 units off production line for QC check | During production | $0 (part of run) |
| **TOTAL** | | **8-14 weeks** | **$500-1,100** |

### P2A.6.2: Fit Testing Protocol

Test on 3-5 runners (including yourself) covering at least 3 sizes. For each test:

1. **Static fit check** — stand, squat, lunge, high knee. Check: waistband roll, ride-up, gapping, restriction
2. **Walking/jogging test** — 15-minute walk + jog. Check: phone pocket bounce, liner shift, elastic dig
3. **Trail run test** — 30-60 minute trail run. Check: chafe points (mark with body marker), snap security, DWR performance in rain/sweat, pocket access while running
4. **Post-run assessment** — photograph any red marks/chafe, record snap looseness, photograph sweat/moisture patterns

### P2A.6.3: Compression Verification

Start with comparative testing against a known 15-20 mmHg reference garment (buy 2XU or Skins compression shorts). Formal lab testing through Intertek or SGS Pakistan costs $200-500 per garment and is required before making specific mmHg marketing claims.

### P2A.10.2: QC Protocol — 100% Inspection

At 200-300 units, use 100% inspection — not AQL sampling. AQL General Level II for a 281-500 lot requires pulling 50 samples, which is essentially full inspection anyway.

**Seven QC Checkpoints:**

| # | Checkpoint | When | What to Check | Pass Criteria |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Incoming fabric | Before cutting | GSM verification (kitchen scale + 10x10cm swatch), DWR spray test (pour water — must bead), shade matching between rolls | GSM within ±5%. Water beads at 80+ rating. No shade variation. |
| 2 | Pre-cutting relaxation | 24 hours before cutting | Lay fabric flat on table for 24 hours minimum | Stretch fabrics must relax to prevent post-cut shrinkage |
| 3 | Post-cutting accuracy | After cutting, before sewing | Check 5% of cut pieces against pattern. Measure key dimensions. | Within ±¼" of pattern dimensions |
| 4 | In-line sewing check | Every 50 garments | Stitch density, seam alignment, thread tension, pocket placement | SPI within spec. No puckering. Pockets within ±⅛" |
| 5 | **100% snap pull test** | After snap attachment | **Pull each snap with 15 lbs (6.8 kg) force for 10 seconds** | **Zero snaps pull out. Zero fabric tearing.** This is the #1 critical QC step. |
| 6 | Measurement audit | After assembly | Measure 5 points per garment against spec sheet | All measurements within tolerance |
| 7 | Final visual + functional | Before packing | Zipper cycles (10x), elastic recovery, thread trimming, stain check, symmetry, label placement | Zero visible defects. Zipper smooth. No loose threads. |
