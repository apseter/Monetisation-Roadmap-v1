## P4A.2: PRICING STRATEGY & PSYCHOLOGY

### P4A.2.1: Value-Anchored Positioning (Recommended)

Premium pricing (£60+) demands trust, reviews, and community equity that doesn't exist yet. Rock-bottom pricing triggers quality skepticism for a technical product. The sweet spot is **transparent value**: explain the DTC model eliminates middleman markup, show modularity advantage vs competitors, and use anchor pricing on product pages.

### P4A.2.2: Round Pricing (Not Charm Pricing)

**Use round numbers (£28, not £27.99).** While charm pricing (.99 endings) increases sales ~24% in general retail, .99 endings negatively affect quality perception in performance categories. Germany (key EU market) shows cultural preference for whole numbers. Round prices signal confidence and simplify multi-currency math.

Reserve .99 endings for promotional periods only.

### P4A.2.3: Launch Discount Strategy

**Hold full prices with no site-wide discounts at launch.** Aggressive launch discounting erodes brand perception and trains customers to wait for sales.

Recommended approach:
- **10% welcome discount via email signup** (captures emails, standard DTC practice)
- "Founding Customer" perk: free shipping on first order or branded gift
- **No percentage-off sales in the first 6 months**

## P4A.3: RECOMMENDED PRICE ARCHITECTURE (REVISED)

### P4A.3.1: The Bundle Pricing Problem

The current system bundle at £43 equals exactly £28 + £15 = £43 — **zero savings.** The double-liner bundle at £55 vs £58 separately saves just £3 (5.2%). Research is unambiguous: bundle discounts below 10% often go unnoticed. The optimal bundle discount for DTC products is **10-15%.**

### P4A.3.2: Revised Pricing Table

| Product | Current | **Recommended** | Savings vs Separate | Discount % |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Outer short | £28 / €33 / $35 | **£28 / €33 / $35** | — | — |
| Compression liner | £15 / €17 / $18 | **£15 / €17 / $18** | — | — |
| System bundle | £43 / €50 / $53 | **£39 / €45 / $48** | £4 / 9.3% | ~9% |
| Double liner bundle | £55 / €63 / $68 | **£49 / €57 / $59** | £9 / 15.5% | ~15% |

### P4A.3.3: Good-Better-Best Architecture

- **Good** (£28): Outer only — entry point, feels incomplete ("liner sold separately")
- **Better** (£39): System bundle — the TARGET option, "Most Popular" badge
- **Best** (£49): Double liner — "Best Value" badge, only £10 more than system

The outer at £28 functions as the **decoy** — incomplete without a liner, naturally pushing buyers toward the £39 bundle.

### P4A.3.4: Product Page Display

Lead with the bundle as the hero. Display strikethrough pricing: ~~£43~~ **£39 — Save £4.** Present individual items as "also available separately." Post-purchase upsell: offer second liner at 15-20% off on the thank-you page.

- **Double-liner bundle is the margin champion** at £17.87 (43.8%).

## P4A.8: BUNDLE PRICING PSYCHOLOGY & DECOY STRATEGY

### P4A.8.1: Key Principles

1. **10-15% minimum bundle discount** for customers to perceive savings (below 10% is mentally rounded to zero)
2. **Three-option architecture** (Good/Better/Best) with the middle option as the target — most customers choose the middle
3. **Decoy pricing**: the outer at £28 feels incomplete, creating natural friction toward the £39 bundle
4. **Strikethrough display**: show ~~£43~~ £39 prominently — the savings must be visible
5. **"Most Popular" badge** on system bundle — social proof drives the default choice

### P4A.8.2: Post-Purchase Upsell

After a bundle purchase, offer a second liner at 15-20% off on the thank-you page. Research shows 28% of DTC merchants using post-purchase add-ons drive significant incremental revenue. A "Complete the System" cart prompt when someone adds just the outer creates a natural upgrade path.

## P4A.12: SHOPIFY, VAT & EU TAX SETUP

### P4A.12.1: Shopify Plan

**Start with Shopify Basic at £25/month** (or £19/month annual billing). The 0.3% processing difference between Basic (2.0%) and Grow (1.7%) saves negligible amounts below £5,000 monthly revenue. Always use Shopify Payments — third-party gateways trigger a 2% additional surcharge.

### P4A.12.2: VAT Setup

- Enable "Include sales tax in product price" in Shopify tax settings
- UK law (Price Marking Order 2004) mandates VAT-inclusive B2C pricing
- £28 shelf price includes 20% VAT → ex-VAT revenue = £23.33
- Add "All prices include VAT" in website footer
- Register for UK VAT voluntarily before £90,000 threshold to reclaim VAT on startup costs

### P4A.12.3: Multi-Currency Markets

Set up **3 Shopify Markets:**
1. **UK** (GBP, primary) — all prices set in GBP
2. **Europe** (EUR) — use fixed price lists, not auto-conversion
3. **Rest of World** (USD) — dormant initially

2% currency conversion fee on non-GBP payouts is a permanent margin drag on EU sales.

### P4A.12.4: IOSS Timing Decision

IOSS intermediary costs €100-300/month. With base case projecting ~54 EU orders in Year 1 (30% of 180), the annual IOSS cost (£1,200-3,600) could exceed EU contribution margin (~£545).

**Recommendation:** Delay IOSS registration until EU orders consistently exceed ~20/month. Initially use DAP (customer pays VAT on delivery) with clear checkout communication, or offer DDP through carrier for per-parcel fee.

### P4A.12.5: Accounting

- **Xero Comprehensive (£47/month)** — multi-currency, MTD compliance, HMRC VAT returns
- **A2X (~£15/month)** — automated Shopify→Xero payout reconciliation
- Corporation tax: **19%** (small profits rate) on eventual profit

## P4A.13: FREE SHIPPING ECONOMICS

### P4A.13.1: The Data

- 47-48% of shoppers abandon carts due to unexpected shipping costs
- "Free shipping saving £6.99 is more appealing than a £10 discount" (Wharton research)
- Free shipping on all orders is uneconomic: £2.62 on a £15 liner = 17.5% of revenue

### P4A.13.2: Recommended Thresholds

| Market | Free Threshold | Below Threshold | Why This Number |
|---|---|---|---|
| **UK** | **Free over £40** | £3.49-3.95 | System bundle (£39) nearly qualifies — nudges add-on purchase |
| **EU** | Free over €65-75 | €5.99-6.99 flat | Forces multi-item / bundle orders |

### P4A.13.3: Implementation

- Display persistent "Free UK Shipping Over £40" banner site-wide
- Cart progress bar: "You're £12 away from free shipping!"
- Product page: "Add a liner for just £15 and get free shipping"
- Post-July 2026: add €3+ EU duty surcharge to EU shipping rates

### P4A.13.4: Conversion Rate Benchmarks

| Source | Expected Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| New DTC apparel (Month 1-6) | 1-2% | Cold traffic, building trust |
| New DTC apparel (Month 6-12) | 2-3% | Reviews, returning visitors |

## P4A KEY INSIGHT

> **Build the entire commercial architecture around bundles.** The system bundle should be the default product page, the hero in all marketing, and the unit economics north star. Individual items exist as entry points and upgrade paths, not as primary revenue drivers. At a £15-25 blended CAC target, organic community-building and micro-influencer gifting are not optional — they are the only path to a sustainable LTV:CAC ratio at these price points.
