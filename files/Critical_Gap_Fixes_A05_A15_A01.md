# GAP FIX #4: A05 — SAMPLING TIMELINE BUFFER

## The Problem

P2C assumes sampling completes in 6-9 rounds over 12-20 weeks. There is zero buffer. In real-world apparel development, sampling overruns are the rule, not the exception. A modular snap-attachment system has never been produced by your factory — the learning curve alone adds 2-4 rounds.

If sampling takes 12 rounds instead of 6, you miss the April/May peak trail season by 2-3 months. A September launch means selling trail shorts as temperatures drop and daylight shrinks — a 40-60% reduction in seasonal demand.

## The Revised Sampling Timeline

### Original Timeline (From P2C — No Buffer)

| Phase | Duration | Cumulative |
|---|---|---|
| Pattern development | 2-3 weeks | Weeks 1-3 |
| Counter samples (2-3 rounds) | 4-6 weeks | Weeks 3-9 |
| Fit samples (2-3 rounds) | 4-6 weeks | Weeks 9-15 |
| Pre-production sample | 2-3 weeks | Weeks 15-18 |
| PP approval + production start | 1-2 weeks | Weeks 18-20 |
| **TOTAL** | **12-20 weeks** | |

### Revised Timeline (With Buffer + Parallel Tracks)

| Phase | Duration | Buffer Added | Cumulative | Risk Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pattern development | 2-3 weeks | +1 week | Weeks 1-4 | Commission 2 pattern makers simultaneously for outer + liner |
| Counter samples Round 1-2 | 3-4 weeks | — | Weeks 4-8 | Focus on snap mechanism FIRST — it's the highest-risk component |
| **SNAP DECISION GATE** | 1 week | **NEW** | Week 9 | If snaps don't work after 2 rounds: PIVOT to hook-and-loop (Velcro) attachment. See fallback below. |
| Counter samples Round 3 | 2-3 weeks | — | Weeks 9-12 | Incorporate snap/attachment revisions |
| Fit samples Round 1-2 | 3-4 weeks | +2 weeks | Weeks 12-18 | Test on 3-5 body types simultaneously, not sequentially |
| Fit samples Round 3 (if needed) | 2-3 weeks | Buffer round | Weeks 18-21 | Only needed if significant fit changes required |
| Pre-production sample | 2 weeks | — | Weeks 21-23 | Must match approved fit sample exactly |
| PP approval | 1 week | — | Weeks 23-24 | Go/No-Go gate — if PP fails, add 2-3 weeks |
| **TOTAL WITH BUFFER** | **22-26 weeks** | **+4-6 weeks** | | |

### What This Means for Launch Date

| Sampling Start | Original End (No Buffer) | Revised End (With Buffer) | Launch (Add 4 wk Production + 2 wk Freight) |
|---|---|---|---|
| January 2026 | April/May 2026 | June/July 2026 | **August/September 2026** |
| November 2025 | February/March 2026 | April/May 2026 | **June/July 2026** |
| September 2025 | December/January 2026 | February/March 2026 | **April/May 2026** ✅ |

**To hit an April 2026 launch, sampling should have started September 2025.** If you're starting sampling now (March 2026), the realistic launch is **September-October 2026** — which means pivoting to an autumn/winter trail running strategy.

### The Snap Mechanism Fallback

If the 6-point snap system doesn't work after 2 sampling rounds (Week 9 gate):

| Fallback | Pros | Cons | Switch Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook-and-loop (Velcro) strips** | Simple, proven, any factory can do it | Less premium feel, can catch leg hair | £0 extra (simpler than snaps) |
| **Silicone gripper integration** | No hardware needed, liner grips waistband | Weakest hold, may slip on descents | £0 extra |
| **Loop-and-button** | Clean aesthetic, durable | Slow to attach/detach | +£0.20/unit |
| **Drawstring tunnel** | Liner threads through outer waistband | Unique but unusual | £0 extra |

**Recommended fallback:** Hook-and-loop (Velcro) strips at 4 points on the waistband. It's the safest, most manufacturable option. The snap system is more premium, but if it's causing 4+ weeks of sampling delays, switch to Velcro and revisit snaps for Run 3.

### Parallel Workstreams to Save Time

| Workstream | Can Start Before PP Approval | Time Saved |
|---|---|---|
| **Packaging design + ordering** | Start at Counter Sample Round 2 (Week 6) | 4-6 weeks |
| **Website/product page build** | Start at Fit Sample Round 1 (Week 12) — use fit sample photos | 4-6 weeks |
| **Pre-launch marketing + waitlist** | Start at Counter Sample Round 1 (Week 4) — use sketches/prototypes | 8-12 weeks |
| **Fabric bulk order** | Start at Fit Sample Round 1 approval (Week 14) | 4-6 weeks |
| **Shipping/logistics setup** | Start at PP sample (Week 21) | 2-3 weeks |

**By running parallel workstreams, you claw back 4-6 weeks of the timeline even with the sampling buffer.**

### Launch Season Contingency

| If Launch Hits... | Season Strategy |
|---|---|
| **April-June** ✅ | Peak trail season. Maximum demand. Execute GTM as planned. |
| **July-August** ⚠️ | Late summer — still good. Lean into late-season races (Lakeland Trails Aug/Sep). |
| **September-October** ⚠️ | Autumn trail. Reposition marketing: "Built for mud season." UK trails are year-round. Trail races run Sep-Nov. |
| **November-January** 🛑 | Winter. Delay launch to March. Use winter for content building and community growth. Do NOT launch trail shorts in British winter. |

# GAP FIX #5: A15/B02/B11 — REVENUE DIVERSIFICATION STRATEGY

## The Problem

The entire GTM plan assumes 100% of revenue comes from DTC Shopify sales driven by organic marketing. There is:
- No wholesale channel strategy (B02)
- No plan if organic marketing fundamentally underperforms (A15)
- No plan if paid ads consistently deliver <1x ROAS (B11)
- No marketplace strategy (Amazon, Etsy)
- No in-person/events sales channel

If organic DTC sells only 40-60 units in 6 months (pessimistic but plausible for a brand-new product with zero brand awareness), the business has no backup revenue stream to sustain operations or justify Run 2.

Selling in person at trail running events gives you: 50%+ margin (no shipping cost, no returns, no packaging), immediate cash (card readers or cash), face-to-face customer feedback (invaluable in first 90 days), content and photos for social media, and email signups from every person who stops at your stand.

### Channel 4: Amazon UK (Defer to Month 6+)

Do NOT launch on Amazon before establishing DTC brand identity. Amazon cannibalises DTC margin (15% referral fee + FBA fees) and removes customer relationship. However, at Month 6-9 when brand has reviews and imagery, Amazon provides volume.

**When to activate Amazon:**
- DTC conversion rate consistently >2%
- 20+ Judge.me reviews (import to Amazon)
- Professional product photography ready
- DTC brand identity established (customers search your brand name)

**Amazon pricing:** Same RRP as Shopify (£28/£15/£39). Do NOT discount on Amazon — price parity protects your DTC channel.

### Critical Quality Gates (Before Spending Any Money)

| Gate | When | Pass Criteria | Fail Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **G1: Fabric sample received** | 2-3 weeks after ordering | Hand feel matches spec, GSM verified, stretch verified | Order from backup supplier |
| **G2: DWR spray test** | Test sample fabric | AATCC 22 score ≥80 (water beads and rolls off at 45° tilt) | Reject supplier. Request DWR re-application or switch to Archroma Pakistan office. |
| **G3: Colour matching** | With sample | Fabric colour matches approved swatch within ΔE<2 | Request re-dye or accept if ΔE<3 |
| **G4: Pre-shipment inspection** | Before releasing 70% payment | GSM ±5%, colour match, DWR test, no defects in random 10% check | REJECT shipment. Do NOT release payment. |
| **G5: Pakistan customs clearance** | Fabric arrival in Lahore | Duty correctly assessed, no seizure, documentation matches | Engage customs broker immediately |
