# GAP FIX #2: B15 — CUSTOMER VALIDATION INTERVIEWS

## Why This Matters

We have 53,000 words of strategy built on desk research, competitor analysis, and industry benchmarks. But we have **zero direct evidence that a real UK trail runner would pay £39 for modular trail shorts from an unknown brand.** This gap must be closed before committing £5,000+ to production.

## The Validation Sprint: One Saturday Morning

### Who to Talk To

You need **10 conversations** with people who match your target customer: trail runners in the UK who run on trails at least twice per month and currently buy their own running gear.

### Where to Find Them

| Method | How Many | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| **parkrun** — volunteer at your local parkrun, chat with trail runners after | 3-5 | Easy — runners are friendly post-run |
| **Local running club trail session** — join a session, talk to people | 2-3 | Easy — natural environment |
| **Instagram DM** — message 10 UK trail runners who post gear content | 2-3 | Medium — some won't reply |
| **Friends/family who run trails** | 1-2 | Easy |
| **Reddit r/trailrunning** — post asking for UK trail runners willing to chat for 10 min | 1-2 | Medium |

### The Interview Script (10 Minutes Per Person)

**Opening (30 seconds):**
"Hey, I'm working on a trail running shorts project and would love 10 minutes of your honest opinion. Not selling anything — just want to understand what runners actually think."

**Question 1: Current behaviour (2 minutes)**
"What shorts do you currently run trails in? What brand, roughly how much did you pay, and how long have you had them?"
→ *Records: brand awareness, price anchor, replacement frequency*

**Question 2: Pain points (2 minutes)**
"What's the one thing you'd change about your current trail shorts if you could?"
→ *Records: whether their pain matches our solution (liner, pockets, DWR, sizing)*

**Question 3: Liner preference (1 minute)**
"Do you prefer built-in liner, no liner, or do you wear separate compression shorts underneath?"
→ *Records: modular relevance to this specific person*

**Question 4: The concept test (2 minutes)**
"Imagine a trail short where the outer shell and compression liner are separate pieces that snap together — you can wear either alone or combined. The outer is water-resistant with a full-size phone pocket. Would that concept interest you?"
→ *Records: initial reaction (excited / interested / indifferent / negative)*

**Question 5: Price sensitivity (2 minutes)**
"If the outer shell was £28 and the liner was £15 — so £39 for the complete system with free shipping — how would that feel? Too expensive, about right, or surprisingly affordable?"
→ *Records: price reaction WITHOUT anchoring to competitors first*

**Question 6: Purchase intent (1 minute)**
"On a scale of 1-10, how likely would you be to try this product from a brand you haven't heard of?"
→ *Records: raw purchase intent. Below 6 average = serious concern.*

**Closing:**
"Thanks so much — if I could add you to a short email list for when this launches, would you be interested?"
→ *Records: email capture (this IS your first waitlist subscribers)*

### GO / NO-GO Decision Rules

After 10 conversations, count the results:

| Signal | GO ✅ | PIVOT ⚠️ | STOP 🛑 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Concept reaction (excited/interested) | 7+ out of 10 | 4-6 out of 10 | 0-3 out of 10 |
| Average purchase intent | 7+ average | 5-6.9 average | Below 5 average |
| Price reaction (about right / affordable) | 7+ out of 10 | 4-6 out of 10 | 0-3 out of 10 |
| Pain point matches modular solution | 6+ out of 10 | 3-5 out of 10 | 0-2 out of 10 |
| Emails captured for waitlist | 5+ | 2-4 | 0-1 |

**If GO:** Proceed with full confidence. Your interviews just de-risked the entire project.

**If PIVOT:** The concept has potential but the specific execution needs adjustment. Common pivots: adjust price point, change target (road runners vs trail), modify the modular mechanism (magnetic vs snap), or reposition as ultra-distance-specific.

**If STOP:** The market doesn't want this product at this price from an unknown brand. Save your £10-25K. This is the most valuable possible outcome of a Saturday morning — it's infinitely cheaper than manufacturing 300 units nobody wants.

# GAP FIX #3: D01 — BRAND NAME SELECTION

## The Blocker

The brand name decision blocks: trademark filing (P1B), domain purchase (P1B), social handle registration (P1B), logo design (P1D), Shopify store setup (P1E), all packaging (P2A/P3A), all marketing materials (P5A), email domain setup (P8A), and legal documentation (P9A).

**Every week the name is unselected delays the ENTIRE project by one week.**

## Decision Framework

From P1A, you have 50 brand name candidates scored on the SMILE framework (Suggestive, Memorable, Imagery, Legs, Emotional). The top 10 were shortlisted.

### 5-Step Name Selection Process (Complete in 1 Week)

**Step 1 (Day 1): Shortlist to 5 names**
Review the P1A document. Pick your personal top 5 based on gut reaction + SMILE scores. Don't overthink — your emotional response matters.

**Step 2 (Day 2): Availability screening (2 hours)**
For each of your 5 names, check:
- [ ] .com domain available? (check Namecheap)
- [ ] .co.uk domain available?
- [ ] Instagram handle available? (or close variation)
- [ ] TikTok handle available?
- [ ] UKIPO trademark search (gov.uk/search-for-trademark) — no identical matches in Class 25?
- [ ] EUIPO trademark search (euipo.europa.eu/eSearch) — no identical matches?
- [ ] Google search — no major existing brand with this name?

**Eliminate any name that fails on .com domain OR trademark availability.** These are non-negotiable.

**Step 3 (Day 3-4): Test with 5 runners**
Share your remaining 2-3 names with 5 trail runners (from your validation interviews above) and ask:
- "Which name do you find most memorable?"
- "Which sounds most like a trail running brand?"
- "Would you feel comfortable wearing this brand name?"
- "Any negative associations?"

**Step 4 (Day 5): Make the decision**
You now have: personal preference + availability data + runner feedback. **Pick the name.** There is no perfect name. There are many good-enough names. The best name is the one you commit to and build equity behind.

**Step 5 (Day 5-7): Secure everything immediately**
Within 24 hours of deciding:
- [ ] Purchase .com and .co.uk domains
- [ ] Register Instagram handle
- [ ] Register TikTok handle
- [ ] Create Strava Club with the name
- [ ] Register Twitter/X handle (even if you don't plan to use it — defensive)
- [ ] File UKIPO trademark application (£170, Class 25)
- [ ] File EUIPO trademark application (€850, Class 25) — or defer 30 days if budget is tight

**Total cost: £200-250 for domains + handles + UKIPO filing.** EUIPO (€850) can follow within 30 days.
