## P6A.1: CUSTOMER SERVICE SETUP FOR A SOLO FOUNDER

### P6A.1.1: Channel Strategy

At <500 orders/month, you do NOT need omnichannel support. Start with the minimum viable setup:

| Channel | Launch? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Email (hello@brand.com)** | Yes — PRIMARY | Professional, async, documented, searchable |
| **Instagram DMs** | Yes — SECONDARY | Customers will DM regardless; monitor daily |
| **Shopify Inbox (live chat)** | No — defer | Creates expectation of instant response you can't sustain solo |
| **WhatsApp Business** | No — defer | Blurs personal/business boundaries for solo founder |
| **Phone** | No — never | Time sink, impossible to scale, no documentation |

### P6A.1.2: Response Time Targets

| Priority | Target | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-purchase sizing questions | <4 hours during business hours | "Will this fit my 34" waist?" |
| Order status inquiries | <12 hours | "Where is my order?" |
| Return/exchange requests | <24 hours | "Wrong size, need to exchange" |
| General inquiries | <24 hours | "Do you ship to Germany?" |
| Complaints/defects | <4 hours | "The snap broke on first use" |

### P6A.1.3: Service Hours

**Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 6 PM UK time.** Set this expectation in your email auto-reply and website footer. Check Instagram DMs once at 9 AM and once at 5 PM. Do NOT check email/DMs on weekends unless you choose to — boundary-setting from Day 1 prevents burnout.

### P6A.1.4: Auto-Reply Setup

Set up an email auto-responder (via Gmail or your email provider):

*"Thanks for reaching out to [Brand]! We've received your message and will reply within 24 hours (usually much faster). In the meantime, you might find your answer here: [link to FAQ page]. For order tracking, click here: [link to Shopify order status page]. — [Your name], Founder"*

### P6A.1.5: FAQ Page (Deflects 40-60% of Inquiries)

Build a Shopify FAQ page covering these exact questions (the top 10 inquiries every shorts brand receives):

1. What size should I order? → Link to size guide + Kiwi Sizing quiz
2. How does the modular system work? → Photo/GIF showing snap attachment
3. Do I need the outer AND the liner? → Explain each works independently
4. How do I wash the shorts/liner? → Care instructions
5. What's the return policy? → 30 days, unworn, tags on
6. How long does shipping take? → UK 2-3 days, EU 5-7 days
7. Do you ship to [country]? → UK + EU yes, rest of world coming soon
8. Are the shorts waterproof? → DWR = water-resistant, not waterproof
9. Will my phone fit in the pocket? → 170×85mm fits all current flagships
10. What's the compression level of the liner? → 15-20 mmHg, comparable to athletic compression

### P6A.1.6: Tools

**Months 1-3: Gmail + Shopify notifications.** Free, simple, sufficient at <100 orders. Create a dedicated support@brand.com or hello@brand.com forwarding to your Gmail. Use Gmail labels: "Sizing," "Returns," "Shipping," "Feedback," "Urgent."

**Month 3+: Tidio (free tier, upgrade at $29/month).** Adds live chat widget, AI chatbot for FAQ deflection, Shopify integration, and unified inbox. Better than Gorgias ($50/month minimum) or Zendesk ($19/agent/month) at your scale.

**Skip entirely:** Gorgias, Zendesk, Intercom — all too expensive and complex for <500 orders/month.

## P6A.3: DAILY ORDER FULFILLMENT WORKFLOW

### P6A.3.1: The Daily Routine (Process Orders Once Per Day)

| Time | Task | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| 9:00 AM | Check Shopify for new orders. Check email for urgent support. | 10 min |
| 9:15 AM | **Batch print shipping labels** via Sendcloud for all orders received since last processing. | 10-15 min |
| 9:30 AM | **Pick and pack** all orders (see P6A.4 for packing station workflow). | 3-5 min per order |
| 10:30 AM | **Drop off** all parcels at nearest Evri ParcelShop or Post Office (batch drop = one trip). | 20-30 min |
| 11:00 AM | Mark all orders as "Fulfilled" in Shopify. Tracking numbers auto-sync from Sendcloud. | 5 min |
| 11:10 AM | Reply to customer service emails. | 15-30 min |
| 11:30 AM | Done with operations — rest of day for marketing/content/brand building. | — |

**Total daily operations time:** 1.5-2.5 hours for 5-15 orders. During launch week (potentially 20+ orders/day), budget 3-4 hours.

### P6A.3.2: Order Processing Frequency

| Daily Orders | Process | Drop-off |
|---|---|---|
| 1-5 | Once daily (morning) | Morning batch |
| 5-15 | Once daily (morning) | Morning batch |
| 15-30 | Twice daily (9 AM + 3 PM) | Morning + afternoon |
| 30+ | **Time to get 3PL quotes** | Unsustainable solo |

### P6A.3.3: Handling Backorders & Out-of-Stock

When a specific size/colour sells out:
1. **Immediately** update Shopify to "Continue selling when out of stock" = OFF for that variant
2. Install **Back in Stock: Restock Alerts** app (free by Swym) — customers enter email for restock notification
3. Add "Sold Out — Notify Me" button on the variant
4. Post on Instagram Stories: "Size M Slate Black sold out! Restock in [X] weeks"
5. Email your waitlist with the specific sizes still available

## P6A.5: BATCH PROCESSING & SHIPPING LABELS

### P6A.5.1: Sendcloud Workflow

1. Open Sendcloud dashboard — all new Shopify orders auto-import
2. Select all unfulfilled orders → "Create Labels"
3. Choose carrier: Evri ParcelShop (UK) or Evri International (EU)
4. Confirm weights (pre-set default: 350g for single item, 500g for bundle)
5. Print all labels at once (thermal printer, 4×6" format)
6. Cut and apply to mailers
7. Sendcloud auto-syncs tracking numbers back to Shopify + sends customer notification

### P6A.5.2: Alternative: Royal Mail Click & Drop

If using Royal Mail instead of Sendcloud:
1. Go to Click & Drop (parcel.royalmail.com)
2. Import Shopify orders via integration
3. Select service (Tracked 48 for UK, International Tracked for EU)
4. Print labels
5. Drop at Post Office counter — they scan all barcodes in one batch

### P6A.5.3: Customs Documentation for EU Orders

Every UK→EU parcel requires a CN22 customs declaration. Sendcloud auto-generates this. Ensure your Shopify products have:
- HS code set (6203.43 for men's woven shorts, 6204.63 for women's)
- Country of origin: Pakistan
- Product descriptions: "Men's trail running shorts, synthetic fibre" (not "clothing" — be specific)
- Declared value: match invoice (do NOT undervalue — this is illegal and risks seizure)

## P6A.6: INTERNATIONAL ORDERS (EU/FRANCE)

### P6A.6.1: EU Order Processing Checklist

- [ ] Customs declaration auto-generated by Sendcloud with HS code + origin
- [ ] IOSS number on label (when registered — see P4A.12.4 for timing)
- [ ] Product description in English (acceptable for customs)
- [ ] Commercial invoice printed and affixed outside parcel (or digital via carrier)
- [ ] VAT collected at checkout via Shopify Markets (France 20%, Germany 19%)

### P6A.6.2: EU Customer Communication

Add to order confirmation email for EU customers: "Your order is shipping from the UK. Estimated delivery: 5-7 business days. All taxes and duties are included in your order total — you will NOT be charged anything additional on delivery."

If NOT yet IOSS registered, instead communicate: "Please note: as your order ships from the UK, local import VAT may be collected by the carrier on delivery. This is standard for cross-border shipments and is required by EU law."

## P6A.7: LOST & DAMAGED PARCELS

### P6A.7.1: Evri Claims Process

| Scenario | Customer says... | Your action |
|---|---|---|
| Not delivered after 7 business days | "My order hasn't arrived" | Check Evri tracking. If stuck >5 days, file claim at evri.com/claim. Reship or refund customer immediately — don't make them wait for claim resolution. |
| Delivered but not received | "I didn't get it" | Check Evri photo POD. If POD shows delivery, share with customer. If unclear, reship or refund. |
| Arrived damaged | "Package was crushed/wet" | Ask for photos. Reship immediately. File Evri claim separately. |
| Wrong item | "I got the wrong size" | Apologise profusely. Ship correct item immediately with prepaid return label for wrong item. |

### P6A.7.2: Compensation Limits

Evri standard compensation: **£20 per parcel** (no enhanced cover on ParcelShop service). Royal Mail Tracked 48: **£20 compensation**. For a £39 bundle, this doesn't cover the full value. Accept this risk at 300 units — buying enhanced cover (~£1-2 per parcel) isn't worth it at your volume. Budget for **2-3% loss rate** (6-9 parcels out of 300).

### P6A.7.3: Decision Rule

**Always side with the customer.** At 300 units, a single negative review or social media complaint causes more damage than the £39 cost of a replacement. Reship first, file claims later. Track losses in a spreadsheet — if loss rate exceeds 5%, switch carriers.

## P6A.8: RETURNS PROCESSING — STEP-BY-STEP

### P6A.8.1: Return Request Workflow

| Step | Action | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Customer emails requesting return | Gmail / Shopify notification |
| 2 | Verify: within 30 days? Order confirmed? | Check Shopify order date |
| 3 | Send return instructions + pay-on-use label | Email template P6A.2.3 |
| 4 | Customer ships return | Evri ParcelShop or Post Office |
| 5 | You receive return (3-5 business days) | Check daily |
| 6 | **Inspect** (see P6A.8.2) | Visual + smell + tag check |
| 7 | Decision: restock or write off | Based on inspection |
| 8 | Process refund in Shopify | Shopify → Orders → Refund |
| 9 | Send refund confirmation email | Shopify auto-sends |
| 10 | Log return reason in spreadsheet | Google Sheets tracker |

### P6A.8.2: Inspection Criteria

| Check | Restock ✅ | Write Off ❌ |
|---|---|---|
| Tags | Still attached | Removed or cut |
| Odour | None / fresh | Body odour, detergent smell, perfume |
| Fabric condition | Clean, no pilling, no stains | Sweat marks, mud, pilling, deodorant marks |
| Snaps | All functional, no deformation | Bent, loose, missing |
| Liner | Unworn appearance, no stretch-out | Stretched, worn appearance, hair |
| Packaging | Original polybag or equivalent | No packaging |

**If in doubt, write off.** Never reship a potentially worn item — one customer receiving a used-looking return destroys trust permanently.

### P6A.8.3: Restocking Process

1. Re-fold garment professionally
2. Place in new polybag (keep spare polybags)
3. Reattach size sticker if removed
4. Return to correct shelf position
5. Update Shopify inventory: +1 to relevant SKU
6. Mark as "restocked" in returns spreadsheet

### P6A.8.4: Return Reason Tracking

Create a Google Sheet with columns: Order Number, Date, SKU, Size, Return Reason (dropdown: Too Small / Too Large / Wrong Size Ordered / Didn't Like Fit / Quality Issue / Changed Mind / Defect / Other), Restocked Y/N, Notes.

**After 30 returns, analyse patterns.** If >40% are "Too Small" on a specific size, your grading may be off. If >20% cite snap issues, investigate production quality. This data drives Run 2 improvements.

## P6A.9: MODULAR SYSTEM RETURNS (PARTIAL RETURNS)

### P6A.9.1: The Unique Challenge

Customer buys £39 bundle, wants to return only the liner (£15) and keep the outer. Or vice versa. This is the modular system's operational complexity.

### P6A.9.2: Policy

**Allow partial returns on bundles.** Refund the component price (£15 for liner, £28 for outer), NOT the discounted bundle equivalent. This means a customer who bought the £39 bundle and returns the liner gets £15 back, keeping the outer for an effective £24 — which is fine (you still made a sale).

### P6A.9.3: Shopify Implementation

Use Shopify's partial refund feature: Orders → select order → Refund → enter line item and amount manually. Add an order note: "Partial return — liner returned, outer kept. Refunded £15."

## P6A.10: EXCHANGE PROCESS

### P6A.10.1: Recommended Approach

**Ship the new size IMMEDIATELY, before receiving the return.** This is operationally generous but creates exceptional customer experience — and the customer is far more likely to actually return the wrong size when they already have the right one.

### P6A.10.2: Workflow

1. Customer requests exchange (e.g., M → L)
2. Check stock of requested size — if available:
3. Create a new Shopify order manually (£0, "exchange" tag) for the replacement
4. Ship replacement same day
5. Email customer with return instructions for original item
6. When original arrives, inspect and restock
7. If original never returned within 14 days, send reminder email. After 21 days, accept the loss.

### P6A.10.3: Tracking Exchanges

Tag all exchange orders in Shopify with "Exchange" tag. Track: original order number, exchange reason, new size, return received Y/N. This data directly informs size guide improvements.

## P6A.11: REVIEW COLLECTION STRATEGY

### P6A.11.1: Tool Selection

| App | Cost | Reviews Included | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Judge.me (Free)** | £0 | Unlimited | **Launch — no reason to pay for reviews at <500 orders** |
| Stamped.io | From $23/mo | Limited | Upgrade path when scaling |
| Loox | From $9.99/mo | Photo/video focus | If visual UGC is priority |
| Yotpo | From $79/mo | Enterprise | Overkill at this scale |

**Install Judge.me Free immediately.** It includes: automatic post-purchase review request emails, photo + video review collection, star ratings on product pages, rich snippet Schema markup for Google (star ratings in search results), and Shopify integration.

### P6A.11.2: Review Request Timing

| Email | Timing | Subject | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 7 post-delivery | "How are the shorts performing?" | Soft check-in, not a hard ask |
| 2 | Day 14 post-delivery | "Your trails are our R&D — share your experience?" | Primary review request |
| 3 | Day 28 (if no review) | "Got 60 seconds? Your review helps fellow runners" | Gentle follow-up |

**Why Day 14:** Trail runners need time to actually run in the shorts. A Day-3 review request gets "they arrived, look nice" — useless. Day 14 gets "ran 40km in these across the Peak District in rain — here's what I found."

### P6A.11.3: Encouraging Photo/Video Reviews

- In the review request email: "Bonus: snap a trail photo wearing your shorts and we'll feature you on our Instagram!"
- On the review form: make photo upload prominent, not buried
- Offer entry into a monthly "Best Trail Photo" giveaway (prize: free liner or accessory)
- NEVER offer discounts for reviews — this violates UK ASA guidelines and Judge.me policies. Offering entry into a giveaway is acceptable.

### P6A.11.4: Benchmarks

| Metric | First 90 Days Target |
|---|---|
| Review request → review conversion | 5-15% (industry average ~8%) |
| Reviews collected (from 100 orders) | 8-15 reviews |
| Average star rating | 4.2+ (below 4.0 = product issue) |
| Photo reviews (% of total) | 20-30% |

## P6A.12: THE ZERO-REVIEWS LAUNCH PROBLEM

### P6A.12.1: The Challenge

Day 1: zero reviews, zero social proof, zero trust. Conversion rate for products with 0 reviews is **~2.5x lower** than products with 5+ reviews. You need social proof FAST.

### P6A.12.2: Solutions (In Priority Order)

1. **Founding Ambassador reviews (Day 1).** Your 20-25 seeded influencers/ambassadors should post reviews on launch day. Ask 5-10 of them to submit Judge.me reviews with photos. This gives you 5-10 reviews on Day 1.

2. **Founder's review.** Write an honest, transparent "Founder's Note" on the product page — not a fake review, but a personal testimony. "I've tested these on 200km of trails across the Peak District. Here's what I learned..."

3. **Pre-launch beta testers.** If you gave samples to friends/family who are runners, ask them for honest Judge.me reviews pre-launch.

4. **Social proof alternatives while reviews build:** display Instagram feed on product page (Instafeed app, free), show "X people are viewing this" (FOMO notification), add "As tested by [ambassador names]" with their photos, and display "Founding 300 — [X] sold" counter.

5. **Prioritise getting to 10 reviews FAST.** After 10 reviews, conversion rate plateaus — the jump from 0→10 matters more than 10→100. Email your first 20 customers personally asking for reviews.

## P6A.13: QUALITY FEEDBACK LOOP — CUSTOMERS TO FACTORY

### P6A.13.1: The Feedback Chain

Customer feedback → Founder analysis → Categorised defect log → Factory communication → Run 2 improvements.

This is the single most valuable operational process for a first-run product. Your first 50 customers are effectively paid beta testers. Every piece of feedback is product development intelligence.

### P6A.13.2: Collection Methods

| Method | When | What It Captures |
|---|---|---|
| Post-purchase survey (email, Day 14) | After sufficient wear time | Fit, comfort, performance, snap quality |
| Review content (Judge.me) | Day 14-28 | Public sentiment, specific praise/complaints |
| Customer service interactions | Ongoing | Issues customers bother to write about (= most serious) |
| Ambassador feedback (direct DM/email) | Week 2-4 | Detailed, technical feedback from serious runners |
| Return reason data | Ongoing | Sizing patterns, quality failure modes |

### P6A.13.3: Weekly Feedback Analysis (15 Minutes)

Every Friday, review:
1. All new reviews — categorise as Fit / Quality / Feature / Positive
2. All return reasons this week — update return tracker
3. All customer service tickets mentioning product issues
4. Any ambassador DMs with feedback

Compile into a running "Product Feedback Log" (see P6A.15).

## P6A.14: POST-PURCHASE SURVEY DESIGN

### P6A.14.1: Send via Klaviyo at Day 14 Post-Delivery

Subject: *"Quick feedback on your trail shorts? (2 minutes)"*

**Questions (use Typeform or Google Forms, link in email):**

1. Which product(s) did you purchase? (Outer only / Liner only / System bundle / Double bundle)
2. How many trail runs have you done in the shorts so far? (0 / 1-3 / 4-10 / 10+)
3. How would you rate the overall fit? (1-5 scale: Much too small → Much too large)
4. How would you rate comfort during running? (1-5: Very uncomfortable → Excellent)
5. Did you experience any chafing? (No / Yes, slight / Yes, significant — where?)
6. How does the phone pocket perform? (Bouncy / Slight movement / Rock solid)
7. If you bought the system: how easy are the snaps to use? (1-5 scale)
8. If you bought the system: did the liner stay attached during your run? (Yes always / Came loose once / Came loose multiple times)
9. How does the DWR perform in rain? (Haven't tested / Water beaded off / Partially absorbed / Fully absorbed)
10. What would you improve about the shorts? (Open text — **most valuable question**)
11. Would you recommend these to a trail running friend? (1-10 NPS scale)
12. Anything else you'd like us to know? (Open text)

**Expected response rate:** 15-25% of recipients (if email is short with clear CTA).

## P6A.16: REORDER DECISION FRAMEWORK

### P6A.16.1: When to Trigger (From P3A)

| Daily Sales Rate | Reorder Trigger | Urgency |
|---|---|---|
| >3 units/day | **Week 2-3 post-launch** | CRITICAL — 10-14 week lead time leaves zero margin |
| 2-3 units/day | Week 4-5 | HIGH — stock lasts ~100-150 days but reorder takes 70-100 days |
| 1-2 units/day | Week 6-8 | MODERATE — monitor trend before committing |
| <1 unit/day | Do not reorder yet | Evaluate product-market fit first |

### P6A.16.2: Run 2 Sizing Decision

Use actual sales data to adjust size ratios:

| Size | Run 1 Allocation | Actual Sales % (example) | Run 2 Adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| S | 11% | 8% | Reduce to 8% |
| M | 25% | 30% | Increase to 30% |
| L | 33% | 32% | Hold at 32% |
| XL | 24% | 22% | Reduce to 22% |
| 2XL | 7% | 8% | Increase to 8% |

**Also factor in:** which sizes sell out first (increase), which sizes have highest return rates (investigate fit), and which sizes have the most waitlist signups (unmet demand).

### P6A.16.3: Run 2 Volume Decision

| Signal | Recommended Volume |
|---|---|
| Run 1 sold out in <60 days | 500-750 units (2-2.5x) |
| Run 1 sold out in 60-120 days | 500 units (1.7x) |
| Run 1 sold 70-90% in 120 days | 400-500 units |
| Run 1 sold <50% in 120 days | Do NOT reorder same product. Investigate why. |

### P6A.16.4: Run 2 Product Changes

Based on feedback log (P6A.15), include in Run 2 tech pack revision:
- Critical fixes (mandatory)
- Moderate improvements (if cost increase <5%)
- Defer minor changes to Run 3
- Consider adding 1 new colourway if customers requested it in surveys

## P6A.17: MANAGING THE STOCKOUT GAP

### P6A.17.1: The Problem

If Run 1 sells well and you reorder at Week 3, Run 2 arrives at Week 13-17. You have ~60-100 days of stock but a 70-100 day lead time. A stockout of 2-6 weeks is likely for popular sizes.

### P6A.17.2: Stockout Management Playbook

| Week | Stock Level | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1-4 | 70-100% | Normal operations. Monitor velocity daily. |
| Weeks 5-8 | 40-70% | Remove paid ads (don't spend money sending traffic to out-of-stock sizes). Focus marketing on available sizes. |
| Weeks 8-10 | 10-40% | Enable "Notify Me" on sold-out variants. Post "Limited Stock" messaging. Email waitlist with urgency. |
| Weeks 10-14 | 0-10% (select sizes OOS) | Accept pre-orders with clear delivery dates ("Ships in 4-6 weeks"). Keep marketing light — focus on content, not conversion. |
| Run 2 arrival | Restock | "Back in Stock" email blast to all waitlist signups. Social media announcement. Resume paid ads. |

### P6A.17.3: Pre-Order Setup on Shopify

Enable "Continue selling when out of stock" for sold-out variants. Add a clear note on the product page: "PRE-ORDER — Ships by [specific date]. All pre-orders ship free." Collect payment at time of order (standard Shopify behaviour). Fulfil pre-orders first when Run 2 arrives.

## P6A.23: LEGAL & COMPLIANCE POST-LAUNCH

### P6A.23.1: UK Consumer Rights Act 2015 Obligations

| Right | Your Obligation | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **Right to reject** | Full refund for faulty goods | Within 30 days of purchase |
| **Right to repair/replace** | Offer repair or replacement for faulty goods | 30 days to 6 months after purchase |
| **Right to price reduction** | If repair/replacement fails | After failed repair attempt |
| **Goods must be satisfactory quality** | No defects, fit for purpose, durable | At time of delivery |
| **Goods must match description** | Product must match what's on your website | Ongoing |
| **Cooling-off period** | Customer can cancel for any reason | 14 days from delivery (distance selling) |

### P6A.23.2: GDPR Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Privacy policy published (from P1G)
- [ ] Cookie consent banner active (CookieYes or similar)
- [ ] Data processing records maintained (what data, why, how long)
- [ ] Customer data deletion process: if customer requests deletion, remove from Klaviyo + Shopify customer data within 30 days
- [ ] Breach notification: if data breach occurs, notify ICO within 72 hours
- [ ] Marketing consent: EU subscribers must double opt-in via Klaviyo

### P6A.23.3: Record-Keeping Requirements

UK Ltd companies must keep:
- All sales invoices and receipts (Shopify auto-generates)
- All purchase invoices (fabric, trims, freight)
- VAT records for 6 years
- Bank statements
- Employee/contractor records (if/when hired)
- Product safety records (REACH test reports, DWR certification)

### P6A.23.4: Product Liability Insurance

**Get product liability insurance before launch.** Athletic apparel carries specific risks: allergic reactions to fabric/DWR chemicals, snap failure causing injury, compression garment complications. Policy cost: **£100-300/year** for a micro DTC brand. Providers: Hiscox, Superscript, Simply Business (all offer online quotes in minutes). Minimum cover: £1M public/product liability.

## P6A.24: PRODUCT LIABILITY & INCIDENT RESPONSE

### P6A.24.1: If a Customer Reports Injury

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Take the report seriously. Express concern. Do NOT admit fault. | Immediate |
| 2 | Ask for full details: what happened, when, where, severity, photos | Within 4 hours |
| 3 | Offer immediate practical help: full refund, replacement, cover reasonable medical costs | Same day |
| 4 | Document everything in writing (save all emails) | Ongoing |
| 5 | Notify your insurance provider | Within 24 hours |
| 6 | If serious injury: consult a solicitor before further communication | Within 48 hours |
| 7 | Investigate root cause: is this a product defect or user-specific reaction? | Within 1 week |
| 8 | If defect confirmed across multiple units: consider voluntary recall | As needed |

### P6A.24.2: Voluntary Recall Process (300 Units)

At 300 units, a recall is operationally manageable:
1. Email all customers who purchased the affected product
2. Explain the issue transparently
3. Offer full refund OR replacement with corrected product
4. Provide prepaid return labels
5. Post a notice on the website
6. Report to local Trading Standards if the defect poses safety risk

## P6A.26: FOUNDER WELLBEING & BURNOUT PREVENTION

### P6A.26.1: The Solo Founder Reality

Running every function of a DTC brand alone is unsustainable beyond 3-6 months. The goal is NOT to do everything forever — it's to learn every function so you can hire/delegate intelligently.

### P6A.26.2: Boundary Rules

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| **No email/DMs after 7 PM** | Customers can wait until 9 AM. You cannot run on no sleep. |
| **One full day off per week** | Saturday OR Sunday is non-negotiable. Batch Friday's work to cover. |
| **Process orders once daily, not continuously** | Checking Shopify every 30 minutes is addictive and unproductive. |
| **Celebrate milestones** | First order, 10th order, first review, first repeat customer — acknowledge wins. |
| **Set a "good enough" standard** | Perfection in packaging, email copy, social posts = burnout. 80% quality at 2x speed beats 100% quality. |

### P6A.26.3: Time Allocation Target (20-25 hrs/week)

| Activity | Hours/Week | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Order fulfillment + shipping | 5-8 | Declines as volume stabilises |
| Customer service | 3-5 | Declines as FAQ page improves |
| Marketing + content | 6-8 | The growth engine — protect this time |
| Operations + admin | 2-3 | Finance, inventory, planning |
| Product development | 2-3 | Feedback analysis, Run 2 planning |
| **TOTAL** | **18-27** | |

If operations consistently exceed 30 hrs/week, you're past the self-fulfillment ceiling. Get 3PL quotes and hire a VA.

## P6A KEY INSIGHT

> **Your first 50 customers are your most valuable asset — not for revenue, but for intelligence.** Every review, return, survey response, and support email contains product development data that's impossible to get any other way. Build systems to capture, categorise, and act on this feedback. The brands that iterate fastest between Run 1 and Run 2 are the brands that survive Year 1. The ones that treat Run 1 feedback as noise are the ones that sit on 150 unsold units wondering what went wrong.
