## P9A.2: UK CONSUMER PROTECTION LAW

### P9A.2.1: Consumer Rights Act 2015 — Your Core Obligations

| Right | What It Means | Your Obligation |
|---|---|---|
| **Satisfactory quality** | Goods free from minor defects, safe, durable | QC protocol (P2C) must catch defects before shipping |
| **Fit for purpose** | Trail shorts must perform as trail shorts | DWR must work, snaps must hold, pockets must fit phones |
| **Match description** | Product matches website description and images | Accurate product copy, representative photos |
| **Right to reject** | Full refund for faulty goods within 30 days | Process within 14 days of receiving return |
| **Right to repair/replace** | After 30 days, offer repair or replacement first | Applies for 6 months from purchase |
| **Right to price reduction** | If repair/replacement fails | 6 months to 6 years after purchase |

### P9A.2.2: Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013 (Distance Selling)

| Requirement | Detail | Your Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| **14-day cooling-off period** | Customer can cancel for ANY reason within 14 days of delivery | Your 30-day policy exceeds this (P1G) |
| **Pre-contract information** | Must provide: trader identity, product description, total price incl. taxes, delivery cost, right to cancel | Product pages + checkout + T&Cs |
| **Confirmation of order** | Written confirmation on durable medium | Shopify auto-sends order confirmation email |
| **Refund timeline** | Refund within 14 days of receiving returned goods | Process in Shopify within 2 business days of inspection |
| **Return shipping cost** | You MAY require customer to pay return shipping | Clearly stated in your returns policy |

### P9A.2.3: Price Marking Order 2004

All prices displayed to UK consumers MUST include VAT. No "plus VAT" pricing. Your £28/£15/£39/£49 prices are VAT-inclusive — configured correctly in Shopify (P8A.1).

### P9A.2.4: Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008

Prohibits: misleading descriptions ("waterproof" when product is water-resistant), fake reviews, hidden charges at checkout, bait advertising (advertising at price you can't supply), and aggressive sales practices. Key risk: do NOT describe DWR shorts as "waterproof." Use "water-resistant" consistently.

## P9A.3: EU CONSUMER RIGHTS DIRECTIVE

### P9A.3.1: Key Differences from UK Law

| Requirement | EU Rule | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Cooling-off period | 14 days from delivery (same as UK) | Your 30-day policy covers this |
| If not informed of cancellation right | Cooling-off extends to **12 months** | MUST include cancellation information in pre-sale communications |
| Legal guarantee | **2 years** (minimum) for goods conformity | Longer than UK's 6-month reversal of burden of proof |
| Language | Product info in language of destination country | French for France (mandatory for textile labeling) |
| ADR (Alternative Dispute Resolution) | Must provide link to EU ODR platform | Include in T&Cs: https://ec.europa.eu/consumers/odr |

### P9A.3.2: France-Specific Consumer Protection

French consumer law provides additional protections beyond the EU Directive. Key difference: France requires a **2-year legal guarantee of conformity** during which the seller (not the consumer) bears the burden of proof that the product was not defective at the time of delivery. In practice: if a French customer reports a snap failure within 2 years, you must replace or refund unless you can prove the customer caused the defect.

## P9A.4: PRODUCT SAFETY — UK & EU REGULATIONS

### P9A.4.1: General Product Safety Regulations 2005 (UK)

All consumer products sold in the UK must be **safe**. For apparel, this means: no sharp edges or points (snap hardware must be smooth), no toxic chemicals above regulated limits (REACH compliance), no choking hazard components, and compliance with relevant standards.

### P9A.4.2: EU General Product Safety Regulation (GPSR) — NEW from December 2024

The EU's updated General Product Safety Regulation requires: a responsible economic operator established in the EU for all products sold to EU consumers, product traceability (batch/serial number), and incident reporting via the Safety Gate/RAPEX system.

**Impact on your brand:** You need an **EU-based responsible person** for products sold in the EU. This can be your IOSS intermediary (some offer this service), a fulfilment partner with an EU address, or a dedicated service provider (~€200-500/year). Without this, your products technically cannot be legally sold in the EU.

### P9A.4.3: What's NOT Required

- **CE marking** — NOT required for standard clothing/apparel (only for PPE)
- **UKCA marking** — NOT required for standard clothing
- **Third-party safety testing** — NOT mandatory for non-PPE apparel, but REACH testing is strongly recommended (P2A)

### P9A.4.4: Compression Liner Safety Note

If you market the compression liner with specific mmHg claims (e.g., "15-20 mmHg compression"), be aware this approaches medical device territory. Athletic compression garments are NOT classified as medical devices if marketed for sports performance rather than medical treatment. Never claim the liner "treats" or "prevents" any medical condition (DVT, varicose veins, etc.). Use language like "athletic compression for muscle support" rather than medical terminology.

## P9A.5: TEXTILE LABELING REQUIREMENTS (UK + EU + FRANCE)

### P9A.5.1: UK Textile Products (Labelling and Fibre Composition) Regulations 2012

| Requirement | Detail | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Fibre composition | Percentage by weight, descending order | "92% Polyamide / 8% Elastane" |
| Accuracy | ±3% tolerance per fibre | Industry standard |
| Language | English for UK market | — |
| Permanently attached | Sewn-in label, not removable hang tag | Woven satin care label |

### P9A.5.2: EU Regulation 1007/2011 on Textile Fibre Names

Same fibre composition requirements as UK, PLUS: must be in the language of the member state where sold. For France: use French fibre names.

| English | French |
|---|---|
| Polyamide / Nylon | Polyamide |
| Elastane / Spandex | Élasthanne |
| Polyester | Polyester |

### P9A.5.3: Care Labeling (ISO 3758 — GINETEX)

Care symbols are not legally mandatory in the UK or EU, but are industry standard and expected by consumers. To use official GINETEX care symbols (the internationally recognised wash/bleach/dry/iron/dryclean symbols), you need a GINETEX licence via UKFT (UK Fashion & Textile Association) at ~£250-500/year.

**Alternative:** Use text-only care instructions without GINETEX symbols — legally compliant and avoids the licence fee.

### P9A.5.4: Country of Origin

| Market | Requirement | Your Label |
|---|---|---|
| UK | Not legally required but strongly recommended | "Made in Pakistan" |
| EU | Not legally required for most textiles but expected | "Made in Pakistan" / "Fabriqué au Pakistan" |
| France | Recommended, valued by consumers | "Fabriqué au Pakistan" |

### P9A.5.5: Complete Label Content

Your sewn-in care label should include:
1. Brand name or logo
2. Fibre composition (English + French): "92% Polyamide / 8% Elastane" | "92% Polyamide / 8% Élasthanne"
3. Care instructions (symbols or text)
4. Country of origin: "Made in Pakistan" | "Fabriqué au Pakistan"
5. Size: XS / S / M / L / XL / 2XL
6. France Triman logo (mandatory for products sold in France — see P9A.15)

## P9A.6: REACH CHEMICAL COMPLIANCE

### P9A.6.1: What Is REACH

REACH (Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals) is EU Regulation EC 1907/2006 governing chemical safety. The UK adopted an identical regulation (UK REACH) post-Brexit. As an importer of finished goods into the UK and seller to the EU, you must ensure products comply with BOTH UK REACH and EU REACH.

### P9A.6.2: Your Obligations

| Obligation | Detail | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Restricted substances (Annex XVII) | Textiles must not contain substances above limits | Request REACH test report from fabric supplier AND conduct testing (P2A) |
| SVHC screening | Products must not contain Substances of Very High Concern above 0.1% by weight | Included in REACH testing package |
| Communication obligation | If SVHC >0.1%, must inform customers | Unlikely for synthetic textiles, but test to confirm |
| Record keeping | Maintain test reports for 10 years | Store digitally in Google Drive |

### P9A.6.3: Key Restricted Substances for Textiles

| Substance | Limit | Risk Source |
|---|---|---|
| Azo dyes (releasing carcinogenic amines) | 30 mg/kg | Fabric dyeing |
| Formaldehyde | 75 mg/kg (direct skin contact) | Fabric finishing/resin |
| Nickel (release from metal) | 0.5 µg/cm²/week | Snap buttons — request nickel-free finish |
| PFAS / PFOS / PFOA | Varies (trending toward full ban) | DWR chemistry — your C0 DWR eliminates this risk |
| Heavy metals (Cd, Pb, Cr VI) | Various limits | Dyes and pigments |
| Phthalates | 0.1% individually | Printed/coated elements |

### P9A.6.4: Testing

Commission REACH testing through SGS, Intertek, or Bureau Veritas in Pakistan (P2A). Cost: $300-600 per style. Test BEFORE first production run. Retain reports for 10 years.

## P9A.8: GDPR & DATA PROTECTION

### P9A.8.1: ICO Registration

Register with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) as a data controller. Cost: **£40/year** (for organisations with turnover under £632,000). Register at ico.org.uk/registration. Failure to register is a criminal offence.

### P9A.8.2: Data You Collect and Legal Basis

| Data | Source | Legal Basis | Retention Period |
|---|---|---|---|
| Name, email, address | Shopify order | Contractual necessity | 7 years (tax requirement) |
| Payment details | Shopify Payments (you don't store card data) | Contractual necessity | Processed by Shopify, not stored by you |
| Email (marketing) | Klaviyo signup | **Consent** (opt-in required) | Until unsubscribe |
| Browsing data | Cookies (GA4, Meta Pixel, Clarity) | Consent (via cookie banner) or legitimate interest (analytics) | Per cookie policy |
| IP address | Server logs | Legitimate interest | 26 months (GA4 default) |

### P9A.8.3: UK vs EU GDPR Key Differences

| Requirement | UK GDPR | EU GDPR |
|---|---|---|
| Governing body | ICO | National DPAs (CNIL for France) |
| Marketing consent | Soft opt-in allowed for existing customers | Explicit opt-in required (double opt-in recommended) |
| Cookie consent | Analytics cookies exempt under Data Use and Access Act 2025 | ALL non-essential cookies require consent |
| International transfers | UK adequacy decisions govern | Standard Contractual Clauses for US transfers |
| Breach notification | 72 hours to ICO | 72 hours to relevant DPA |

### P9A.8.4: Klaviyo Consent Configuration

| Market | Opt-In Type | Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| UK | Single opt-in (soft opt-in for customers) | Klaviyo default form |
| EU | Double opt-in (explicit consent required) | Klaviyo → Lists → Settings → Enable double opt-in for EU-located subscribers |

### P9A.8.5: Data Subject Rights Response Process

If a customer requests data access, deletion, or portability:
1. Verify identity (ask them to confirm from the email address on their account)
2. Respond within **30 days** (legally required)
3. For deletion: remove from Klaviyo lists, anonymise in Shopify (retain order data for tax but remove PII)
4. For access: export their Shopify customer data + Klaviyo profile data and send securely
5. Log all requests in a DSAR (Data Subject Access Request) register

Install **CookieYes** (free Shopify app, covers up to 100 pages). Configure:

## P9A.10: ADVERTISING STANDARDS (ASA + CAP CODE)

### P9A.10.1: Key Rules for DTC Apparel Brands

| Rule | Detail | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Truthfulness** | All claims must be substantiated | "Water-resistant" requires DWR test data. "15-20 mmHg" requires lab testing. |
| **Misleading pricing** | "Was" prices must be genuine | Don't inflate pre-sale prices to make discounts look bigger |
| **Environmental claims** | Must be specific, not vague | "PFC-free DWR" is specific and acceptable. "Eco-friendly" is vague and risky. |
| **Comparative claims** | Must be verifiable and fair | "Half the price of Path Projects" is verifiable. "Best trail shorts ever" is subjective. |
| **Testimonials** | Must reflect genuine opinions | Never fabricate reviews. Ambassador posts must be genuine. |

### P9A.10.2: Environmental Claims — The Green Claims Code

The CMA (Competition and Markets Authority) enforces the Green Claims Code. Your claims must be: truthful and accurate, clear and unambiguous, not omit important information, consider the full lifecycle, and be substantiated.

**Safe claims for your brand:** "PFC-free DWR finish," "C0 (zero-fluorocarbon) water repellent," "OEKO-TEX certified fabric" (if certified), "Small-batch, made-to-order" (if true), "Modular design means less waste" (if genuinely reducing consumption).

**Risky claims to avoid:** "Sustainable" (too vague), "Eco-friendly" (unsubstantiated), "Green" (meaningless), "Carbon-neutral" (unless verified by third party), "Biodegradable" (unless the entire product actually is).

## P9A.11: INFLUENCER & AMBASSADOR DISCLOSURE RULES

### P9A.11.1: UK ASA Requirements

| Scenario | Disclosure Required? | How |
|---|---|---|
| Gifted product, no content obligation | **Yes** — #gifted or #ad | Caption or visible in video |
| Gifted product with content expectation | **Yes** — #ad | Must be prominent, not buried in hashtags |
| Paid collaboration | **Yes** — #ad | Clear and prominent |
| Ambassador with discount code earning commission | **Yes** — #ad or #affiliate | Must be obvious |
| Customer sharing genuine UGC (no incentive) | No | Not a commercial relationship |

### P9A.11.2: Key Rules

- Disclosure must be **immediately visible** — not hidden after "more" fold or buried in 30 hashtags
- Instagram's "Paid partnership" tag is acceptable but #ad in caption is also required by ASA
- TikTok's "Sponsored" tag is acceptable
- Applies to ALL platforms including Stories, Reels, TikTok, YouTube, podcasts, and blog posts
- The BRAND is responsible if the influencer fails to disclose — not just the influencer

### P9A.11.3: Your Obligation

Include in every ambassador agreement: "You must clearly disclose the commercial nature of this relationship using #ad or #gifted in every post, Story, Reel, or video featuring [Brand] products. This is a legal requirement under UK ASA rules."

## P9A.15: FRANCE-SPECIFIC COMPLIANCE (LOI AGEC, REFASHION, TRIMAN)

### P9A.15.1: Loi AGEC (Anti-Waste Law for a Circular Economy)

France's Loi AGEC imposes specific obligations on any brand selling products with packaging in France:

| Obligation | Detail | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Refashion registration** | Register as a producer with Refashion (France's textile EPR scheme) | Register at refashion.fr via a French mandataire |
| **Eco-contribution fee** | Pay per-unit fee for textile waste management | ~€0.06-0.08 per garment at your volume |
| **Triman logo** | Display on product or packaging | See P9A.15.2 |
| **Info-Tri sorting instructions** | Display sorting information alongside Triman | See P9A.15.2 |
| **No destruction of unsold goods** | Cannot destroy unsold inventory — must donate or recycle | Relevant at larger scale |

### P9A.15.2: Triman Logo & Info-Tri

Since February 2023, ALL products sold in France must display the **Triman logo** (stylised person + circular arrow) alongside **Info-Tri** sorting instructions telling the consumer how to dispose of the product.

For textiles: "À déposer dans un point de collecte textile" (Deposit at a textile collection point). For packaging: "Emballage → Bac de tri" (Packaging → Recycling bin).

**Where to display:** On the product label, on the packaging, or accessible via QR code on the product/packaging linking to a digital version.

### P9A.15.3: French Mandataire (Representative)

If you're a non-French company selling in France, you need a **mandataire** (representative) to register with Refashion on your behalf. Services like Ecostar, EcoMundo, or ADIFLY offer this for **€300-800/year** including registration + annual declaration + eco-fee calculation.

### P9A.15.4: CITEO Registration (Packaging EPR)

Separate from Refashion (textiles), **CITEO** is France's packaging EPR scheme. If you ship packaged products to French consumers, register with CITEO. Annual eco-fees at your volume: likely **<€50/year**. Register via citeo.com or through your mandataire.
