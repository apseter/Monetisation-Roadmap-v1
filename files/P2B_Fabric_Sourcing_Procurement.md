## P2B.2: SPECIFYING C0 DWR CHEMISTRY

### P2B.2.1: Approved C0 DWR Chemical Brands

Tell your supplier: "We need C0 DWR — completely fluorocarbon-free, no PFAS chemistry." Name specific brands:

| Brand | Product | Chemistry | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Daikin** | UNIDYNE XF | Bio-based (>50%), PFC-free | Market leader. bluesign approved. Excellent wash durability. |
| **Rudolf** | BIONIC-FINISH ECO | Hyperbranched dendrimer | Used by H&M across their range. Well established. |
| **Archroma** | SMARTREPEL Hydro | 100% water-based | PFAS below detection limits. Archroma has Lahore office. |
| **Huntsman** | PHOBOTEX RSY | Hydrocarbon-based | Now under Archroma. Widely available in China. |
| **Wacker** | SILRES WH 120 | Silicone-based | Excellent breathability for athletic use. |
| **CHT** | zeroF | Paraffin-based | Good budget option. |

### P2B.2.2: What to Demand from the Supplier

1. Confirm which DWR brand they apply (exact product name)
2. Provide Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for the DWR chemical
3. Provide AATCC 22 spray test report showing rating ≥80 initial
4. Provide wash durability data (rating after 20 home washes — should be ≥70)
5. Provide PFAS test report showing total fluorine below detection limits

**Mandarin term to include:** 无氟防水 (wú fú fáng shuǐ) = "fluorine-free water repellent" — signals you know what you're asking for.

**Why this matters:** France banned PFAS in consumer textile clothing effective January 2026. Denmark follows July 2026. EU-wide ban expected 2027-2028. Non-compliant fabric = cannot sell in your #2 market.

## P2B.3: QUALITY VERIFICATION WHEN SAMPLES ARRIVE

### P2B.3.1: DIY Testing Protocol (Do These Immediately)

| Test | Method | Pass Criteria | Equipment Needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GSM check** | Cut exact 10×10cm swatch, weigh, multiply by 100 | Within ±5% of stated GSM | Digital scale (0.01g accuracy, ~$10 on Daraz) |
| **Stretch recovery** | Mark 2 points 10cm apart, stretch to 20%, hold 1 min, release, measure | >95% recovery (residual deformation <0.5cm) | Ruler, marker |
| **DWR spray test** | Hold fabric at 45°, pour water slowly | Water beads and rolls off, no absorption | Cup of water |
| **Colorfastness** | Rub damp white cotton cloth vigorously 20x | No significant color transfer | White cloth |
| **Hand feel** | Compare to competitor reference (Patagonia Strider Pro for outer, 2XU for liner) | Comparable quality feel | Competitor product |
| **Opacity** | Hold to bright light | No see-through for outer (especially in light colors) | Lamp or window |

### P2B.3.2: Professional Lab Testing ($300-600)

Send samples to SGS or Intertek in Lahore for:
- GSM verification (certified)
- AATCC 22 spray test (DWR rating)
- Colorfastness to washing (ISO 105-C06)
- Stretch and recovery % (4-way)
- PFAS screening (total fluorine content)

Turnaround: 5-10 business days. This is worth the cost to verify before committing $2,000-5,000 to bulk fabric.

## P2B.4: AVOIDING ALIBABA SCAMS & PROTECTING QUALITY

### P2B.4.1: Red Flags to Watch For

| Red Flag | What It Means | How to Check |
|---|---|---|
| Price 30-40% below all competitors | Quality will be compromised | Get 5+ quotes to establish baseline |
| Overly broad product range | Trading company, not factory | Ask for factory video call showing production floor |
| Cannot do video call from factory | Not the actual manufacturer | Request video with your name + date on whiteboard visible |
| Reluctance to show factory photos | Hiding something | Ask for dated photos with your inquiry number visible |
| Photoshopped certifications | Fake compliance claims | Verify OEKO-TEX at oeko-tex.com/en/label-check, bluesign at bluesign.com |
| Pushes for full payment upfront | Scam risk | NEVER pay 100% upfront. Use Trade Assurance or 30/70 split |
| "We can do any fabric" | No specialization = no expertise | Look for suppliers focused on athletic/performance fabrics specifically |

### P2B.4.2: Verification Steps

1. **Ask for business licence photo** (营业执照) — this is public information in China and every legitimate factory has one
2. **Cross-reference on 1688.com** — China's domestic B2B platform shows actual factory profiles and domestic pricing
3. **Request factory audit report** if they claim to supply major brands (BSCI, WRAP, SA8000 reports)
4. **Always retain sealed counter-samples** — when approving fabric, keep a sealed 50cm sample labeled with supplier name, date, lot number. This is your evidence in any quality dispute.

## P2B.6: PAYMENT TERMS & TRADE ASSURANCE

### P2B.6.1: Recommended Payment for First Order

**Use Alibaba Trade Assurance** for your first fabric order. It works as escrow:

1. You pay to Citibank account designated by Alibaba
2. Funds held until you confirm receipt and quality
3. If fabric doesn't match agreed specs, open dispute with evidence
4. Alibaba mediates and can refund if supplier is at fault

**Coverage:** 100% on-time shipment + 100% product quality protection.

**Limitation:** Suppliers bake 2-3% transaction fee into pricing. Color/hand-feel disputes are subjective and harder to prove.

### P2B.6.2: For Subsequent Orders (After Trust Established)

Switch to **T/T wire transfer with 30/70 split:**
- 30% deposit before production starts
- 70% balance AFTER pre-shipment inspection passes but BEFORE goods ship

This is the global standard for fabric orders. Never pay 100% upfront. Never pay 70% before inspection.

### P2B.6.3: Incoterms

**Use FOB Ningbo or FOB Shanghai.** Under FOB:
- Supplier handles all Chinese-side logistics (inland transport, export customs, port loading)
- You control freight forwarder selection and shipping costs from port onward
- Maximum cost transparency

**Avoid CIF** — suppliers mark up freight costs 15-30%. **Avoid EXW** — handling Chinese export customs requires local expertise.

## P2B.8: PAKISTAN CUSTOMS CLEARANCE & DUTIES

### P2B.8.1: Pre-Requisites for Importing

Before your first import, you need:
1. **National Tax Number (NTN)** from FBR — your factory/company likely has this
2. **Sales Tax Registration Number (STRN)** — register at iris.fbr.gov.pk
3. **WeBOC registration** — Pakistan's electronic customs clearance system
4. **Licensed clearing agent** — handles paperwork at Karachi port ($100-150 per shipment)

### P2B.8.2: Duty Calculation for Synthetic Fabric

| HS Code | Description | Your Fabric |
|---|---|---|
| **5407.42** | Dyed woven nylon fabric, ≥85% nylon | Ripstop nylon outer |
| **6006.32** | Dyed knitted fabric of synthetic fibres | Nylon/spandex liner |

**Estimated duties on a $1,400 CIF fabric shipment:**

| Duty/Tax | Rate | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Customs duty | ~15% | $210 |
| Additional customs duty | ~2% | $28 |
| Regulatory duty (if applicable) | ~8% | $112 |
| Sales tax (GST) | 18% (on CIF + all duties) | ~$315 |
| Income tax (withholding) | 6% | $84 |
| **TOTAL DUTIES & TAXES** | | **~$750** |

### P2B.8.3: China-Pakistan FTA Potential Savings

The China-Pakistan FTA Phase II (effective January 2020) may provide reduced duty rates. However, Pakistan has placed many synthetic textile products on its protected list. Request a **Form P Certificate of Origin** from your Chinese supplier ($20-50, issued by CCPIT) and have your clearing agent verify whether your specific HS code qualifies for preferential rates.

## P2B.9: SUPPLIER NEGOTIATION FOR SMALL ORDERS

### P2B.9.1: Strategies That Work

1. **Order stock colors** — slashes MOQ from 1,000m+ to 100-500m
2. **Frame as trial** — "First order is 500m. We project 2,000-5,000m within 12 months."
3. **Combine fabrics** — order both ripstop and compression from same supplier to increase total value
4. **Accept 10-30% price premium** — a $2.50/m fabric at 3,000m costs $3.00-3.25/m at 300m. This is normal.
5. **Time your order** — slow seasons (post-Chinese New Year Feb-Mar, mid-summer Jul-Aug) improve small-order acceptance
6. **Build relationship** — Chinese suppliers value long-term relationships (guanxi). Be responsive, professional, and consistent.

## P2B.11: PRE-SHIPMENT INSPECTION

### P2B.11.1: Book Inspection Before Paying 70% Balance

Use QIMA ($200-400/man-day) or SGS ($250-500/man-day). For 300-500m of fabric, one man-day is sufficient.

**Inspector checks:**
- Fabric weight/composition vs spec
- Color consistency between rolls and vs approved sample
- Defects per roll (4-point grading system)
- Width conformity
- DWR finish quality (spray test on each roll)
- Roll packaging and labeling

**Combined QC budget (inspection + lab testing): $500-1,000.** This protects a $2,500-4,000 fabric investment.

**NEVER pay the final 70% balance until pre-shipment inspection passes.**
