## Turning First-Time Buyers into Repeat Customers & Brand Advocates
### Modular-Liner Trail Shorts D2C Brand | March 2026 | Confidential

---

> **P10A Subproject Summary:** This document covers the complete customer experience lifecycle from first website visit through repeat purchase, referral, and brand advocacy. Covers the end-to-end CX journey map, unboxing experience design, post-purchase nurture, repeat purchase strategy, loyalty/referral programme, NPS measurement, customer segmentation for personalised marketing, win-back campaigns, community-to-commerce flywheel, and Year 2 product expansion guided by customer data.

> **P10A Core Reality:** At P4A's blended CAC of £15-25, a customer who buys once and never returns is barely profitable. The business model REQUIRES a 30-40% repeat purchase rate within 12 months and an LTV:CAC ratio above 2.5:1. This document is the engine that makes that happen.

> **P10A Key Metric:** Target 30-40% of Year 1 customers making a second purchase within 12 months. At 180 base-case orders, that's 54-72 repeat orders generating an additional £2,100-2,800 in revenue — the difference between a loss-making hobby and a sustainable business.

## P10A.1: END-TO-END CX JOURNEY MAP

### P10A.1.1: The 8 Stages of the Customer Journey

| Stage | Touchpoints | Emotion Goal | Key Metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. **Discovery** | Instagram, TikTok, Google, referral, event, press | Curiosity — "what are modular shorts?" | Impressions, sessions |
| 2. **Consideration** | Product page, size guide, reviews, FAQ | Confidence — "this solves my problem" | Add-to-cart rate |
| 3. **Purchase** | Checkout, payment, confirmation email | Excitement — "I just bought something cool" | Conversion rate, AOV |
| 4. **Waiting** | Shipping notification, tracking | Anticipation — "when does it arrive?" | Delivery time, tracking accuracy |
| 5. **Unboxing** | Package, insert card, product feel | Delight — "this feels premium" | Unboxing UGC, social shares |
| 6. **First Use** | First trail run in the shorts | Validation — "these actually work" | Review rating, return rate |
| 7. **Advocacy** | Review, social post, word of mouth | Pride — "I found something great" | Review count, referral rate |
| 8. **Loyalty** | Repeat purchase, new colourway, referral | Belonging — "I'm part of this brand" | Repeat rate, LTV |

### P10A.1.2: Where Most DTC Brands Fail

Most brands optimise for Stages 1-3 (acquisition) and ignore Stages 4-8 (retention). At your scale, the math is clear: acquiring customer #101 costs £15-25 in marketing. Getting customer #50 to buy again costs £0.50 (one Klaviyo email). **Retention is 30-50x more cost-efficient than acquisition.**

## P10A.2: PRE-PURCHASE EXPERIENCE

### P10A.2.1: Product Page Trust Signals

The product page is where 90% of purchase decisions are made or lost. Beyond the elements in P8A.2.3, add these trust-building elements:

| Trust Signal | Where | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **"Tested on 200km of UK trails"** | Below product title | Founder credibility |
| **"30-day trail guarantee — run in them, return if not convinced"** | Below Add to Cart | Removes risk |
| **"Free UK shipping over £40"** | Announcement bar + product page | Drives bundle purchase |
| **"300 made. First run."** | Product description | Scarcity + exclusivity |
| **Star rating + review count** | Below product title (Judge.me) | Social proof |
| **"As worn by [Ambassador Name]"** | Image carousel or testimonial block | Aspirational proof |
| **Modular demo GIF/video** | Product gallery (position 3-4) | Shows the snap system in action |

### P10A.2.2: Size Guide as CX Tool

The size guide is not just a table — it's the #1 return prevention tool. Your size guide should include: body measurement instructions with visuals (waist, hip, inseam), a recommendation engine ("If your waist is 82cm, order M"), comparison to popular brands ("Similar fit to Nike Dri-FIT Stride in M"), a "Between sizes?" recommendation (size up for outer, size down for liner compression), and fit photos on multiple body types (not just one athletic model).

Install **Kiwi Sizing** (free) for an interactive quiz: "Answer 3 questions → get your size." This alone can reduce size-related returns by 20-40%.

## P10A.3: CHECKOUT & PAYMENT EXPERIENCE

### P10A.3.1: Reducing Checkout Friction

| Friction Point | Solution |
|---|---|
| Account creation required | Guest checkout enabled (P8A.1.2) |
| Surprise shipping cost | Display shipping cost on product page, not just checkout |
| Limited payment options | Shop Pay + Apple Pay + Google Pay + PayPal + Klarna |
| Slow checkout load | Express checkout buttons above standard form |
| Discount code box visible | Keep visible but don't make it prominent — visible empty code boxes cause 27% of shoppers to leave to find a code |
| EU customers hit with unexpected VAT | Use IOSS or clearly state "All taxes included" |

### P10A.3.2: Post-Purchase Upsell (Thank-You Page)

After purchase, the thank-you page is the highest-intent real estate on your site. The customer just committed money — they're in buying mode.

| If Customer Bought... | Show This Upsell | Discount | Expected Take Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Outer only | "Add a liner for 15% off — £12.75 instead of £15" | 15% | 10-15% |
| Liner only | "Complete the system — outer at 15% off — £23.80" | 15% | 8-12% |
| System bundle | "Grab a second liner — 20% off — £12" | 20% | 12-18% |
| Double bundle | "Join our Strava Club for exclusive challenges" | — | 20-30% |

Implement via Shopify's native post-purchase upsell (available on all plans) or the **ReConvert** app (free plan available).

## P10A.4: UNBOXING EXPERIENCE DESIGN

### P10A.4.1: The £0.48-1.13 Unboxing That Feels Like £5

Every package should create a moment worth sharing. At your budget, this doesn't mean luxury packaging — it means thoughtful touches.

| Component | Cost | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Recycled kraft mailer** | £0.25-0.50 | Functional, eco-aligned, protects product |
| **Branded sticker** (seal the mailer) | £0.03-0.08 | First brand touchpoint, closeable moment |
| **Tissue paper** (1 sheet, branded or kraft) | £0.05-0.15 | Adds tactile layer, slows the reveal |
| **Insert card** (A6, double-sided) | £0.08-0.15 | See P10A.4.2 |
| **Care instruction card** (business card size) | £0.03-0.05 | Practical, reduces "how do I wash" CS tickets |
| **Founding 300 card** (Run 1 only) | £0.04-0.10 | "You're one of the Founding 300" exclusivity |

### P10A.4.2: The Insert Card (Most Important Component)

Front side: "Welcome to [Brand]. You're now one of the Founding 300." Photo of founder on a trail. One-line brand story.

Back side: 
- QR code to "How the Modular System Works" video (30 seconds)
- QR code to Strava Club
- "Share your first trail run wearing [Brand] → tag @brand + #RunModular → we'll feature you on our page"
- "Questions? hello@brand.com — I reply personally. — [Founder Name]"

### P10A.4.3: Why Unboxing Matters for Trail Running

Trail runners are gear enthusiasts. They photograph new kit. They post unboxing Stories. **Your package IS marketing content.** Every shareable unboxing moment is free advertising to an audience of trail runners who trust the poster's recommendation.

Target: **10-15% of customers share an unboxing photo or Story** within 48 hours of delivery. Track by monitoring your branded hashtag and tagged posts.

## P10A.6: PRODUCT ONBOARDING — TEACHING THE MODULAR SYSTEM

### P10A.6.1: The Onboarding Problem

The modular snap system is your biggest differentiator but also your biggest CX risk. If a customer can't figure out how to attach the liner, they'll return the product and leave a bad review. **Product onboarding is not optional.**

### P10A.6.2: Onboarding Content

| Content | Format | Where | When Shown |
|---|---|---|---|
| "How to snap in your liner" | 30-second video | QR code on insert card + product page + Day 3-5 email | Unboxing + first email |
| "3 ways to wear your modular shorts" | 60-second Reel/TikTok | Instagram + TikTok + product page | Pre and post-purchase |
| "Outer only / Liner only / Combined" | Photo carousel | Product page + Day 3-5 email | Always visible |
| "What those 6 snaps do" | Diagram | Size guide tab + FAQ | Pre-purchase |
| "Trail running in the rain — does DWR work?" | 30-second video | Blog + email Month 2 | Seasonal content |

### P10A.6.3: Snap Success Metric

Track customer service inquiries about the snap system. If >5% of customers ask "how do I attach the liner?" in Month 1, your onboarding content isn't working. Respond by: making the video more prominent, adding a printed diagram to the insert card, and improving the snap mechanism's intuitiveness for Run 2.

## P10A.9: REPEAT PURCHASE STRATEGY

### P10A.9.1: What Drives Repeat Purchase in Trail Running Apparel

| Driver | How to Activate | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **Replacement need** | Shorts wear out (12-18 month cycle). Remind at Month 12. | Month 12+ |
| **Colour/variety** | New colourway drops. "First access for existing customers." | Run 2+ (Month 6-9) |
| **Product expansion** | New products (long liner, vest, cap) launching. | Year 2 |
| **Gift giving** | "The perfect gift for your trail buddy — they'll know the size." | November-December |
| **Additional liner** | "One in the wash, one on the trail — grab a spare." | Month 1-3 (earliest repeat) |
| **Seasonal need** | Summer = lighter fabric, winter = warmer liner. | Product line expansion |

### P10A.9.2: The Second-Liner Strategy (Fastest Repeat Purchase)

The single most likely repeat purchase is a **second liner**. At £15, it's a low-commitment buy. Position as: "One to wear, one to wash." Target timing: 14-30 days after first purchase (when they've used the first liner 3-5 times and it's in the laundry). Cross-sell email at Day 21 (already in P10A.5.1). Offer 15-20% off second liner for existing customers.

**Expected second-liner attach rate:** 15-25% of bundle buyers within 60 days.

### P10A.9.3: New Colourway Drops (Run 2+)

When Run 2 introduces a new colourway: email existing customers 48 hours before public launch ("You get first pick"), show the new colour alongside their original purchase ("Complete your rotation"), and limit early access to 48 hours to create urgency. Expected uptake: 5-10% of existing customers purchase the new colourway.

### P10A.9.4: Replenishment Reminder (Month 10-12)

At Month 10 post-purchase, send: "Your trail shorts have seen [estimated] km of trails. Time for a fresh pair?" Include a 10% loyalty discount. Trail shorts typically last 12-18 months with regular use — this timing catches the replacement window.

## P10A.11: LOYALTY & REFERRAL PROGRAMME

### P10A.11.1: Referral Programme (Launch Month 2)

**Structure:** "Give £5, Get £5" — customer shares a unique link, friend gets £5 off first order (min £28), customer gets £5 store credit when friend purchases.

**Tool:** UpPromote (free tier) or GoAffPro (free). Integrate with Klaviyo for automated referral emails.

**Expected performance:** 5-8% of customers share their referral link. 10-20% of referred visitors convert. Each referral costs you £10 (£5 to referrer + £5 to friend) but acquires a customer at £10 CAC — far below paid advertising.

### P10A.11.2: Loyalty Programme (Defer to 500+ Customers)

Do NOT launch a points-based loyalty programme at <500 customers. It adds complexity, requires another app (Smile.io at £0-49/mo), and doesn't move the needle at small scale. Instead, create "informal loyalty" through: personal founder emails to repeat buyers, surprise upgrade to next-day shipping for 3rd purchase, handwritten note in 3rd order ("You're officially a trail regular"), first access to new products, and invitation to product development feedback sessions.

Formal loyalty programme (points, tiers) makes sense at **500+ customers, 100+ repeat buyers.**

## P10A.12: NPS & CUSTOMER SATISFACTION MEASUREMENT

### P10A.12.1: NPS Survey (Send at Day 30)

Add to the Day 30 email: "On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend [Brand] to a trail running friend?"

| Score | Category | Follow-Up |
|---|---|---|
| 9-10 | **Promoters** | "Thanks! Would you share your referral link? [link]" → Convert to advocate |
| 7-8 | **Passives** | "What would make us a 10? We're always improving." → Collect feedback |
| 0-6 | **Detractors** | "We're sorry to hear that. Can you tell us what went wrong?" → Personal outreach, resolve issue |

**NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors.** Target: **NPS 50+** (athletic DTC average is 40-60).

### P10A.12.2: When NPS Matters

At 30 customers, NPS is directional, not statistically valid. Start measuring from Day 1 but don't make strategic decisions until you have 50+ responses. Below 30 NPS after 50 responses = serious product or experience issue. Above 60 NPS = strong product-market fit.

### P10A.12.3: Lightweight CSAT (No Survey Fatigue)

Don't send separate CSAT surveys — you'll exhaust your small customer base. Instead, embed satisfaction signals into existing touchpoints: review rating (Judge.me), NPS question (Day 30 email), return rate (operational metric), and repeat purchase rate (behavioural metric). These four data points together paint a complete satisfaction picture without additional survey burden.

## P10A.13: WIN-BACK CAMPAIGNS FOR LAPSED CUSTOMERS

### P10A.13.1: Lapsed Customer Definition

| Days Since Last Purchase | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0-60 | Active | Normal post-purchase nurture |
| 60-90 | Cooling | Strava challenge invite, community content |
| 90-150 | At risk | Win-back flow triggered |
| 150-270 | Lapsed | Aggressive win-back with discount |
| 270+ | Dormant | Final re-engagement attempt, then suppress |

### P10A.13.2: Win-Back Flow (Klaviyo, 4 Emails)

| Email | Day | Subject | Offer |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 90 | "We miss seeing you on the trails" | No discount — community content, Strava Club invite, new blog post |
| 2 | 120 | "Something new just dropped" | New colourway or product announcement (if available) |
| 3 | 150 | "Here's 15% off — just for trail regulars" | 15% discount, 7-day expiry |
| 4 | 180 | "Last chance: 20% off before we say goodbye" | 20% discount. If no engagement → suppress from marketing |

### P10A.13.3: Suppression Policy

After Email 4 with no open/click: move to "Suppressed" segment. Stop sending marketing emails. This protects deliverability (sending to unengaged contacts hurts email sender reputation), reduces Klaviyo costs (you pay per contact), and respects the customer's implicit choice.

## P10A.15: HANDLING NEGATIVE EXPERIENCES

### P10A.15.1: The Service Recovery Paradox

Research consistently shows that customers whose complaints are resolved excellently become MORE loyal than customers who never had a problem. A negative experience is an opportunity to create an advocate — if handled correctly.

### P10A.15.2: Negative Experience Response Framework

| Severity | Example | Response Time | Resolution |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Minor** | Slow delivery (1-2 days late) | 12 hours | Apologise, explain, offer free shipping on next order |
| **Moderate** | Wrong size sent | 4 hours | Ship correct size immediately (before return arrives) + free return label |
| **Serious** | Product defect (snap failure, seam split) | 2 hours | Full replacement + keep defective pair + 20% off next order + personal apology email from founder |
| **Critical** | Injury or allergic reaction | 1 hour | Immediate concern + offer to cover medical costs + report to insurer + investigate root cause |

### P10A.15.3: The "Make It Right" Budget

Set aside **£300/year** (£25/month) for above-and-beyond service recovery: surprise upgrades, free replacements for edge-case complaints, gift cards for customers who had poor experiences. At 180 orders/year, this is £1.67 per customer — a trivial cost that generates disproportionate loyalty and positive word-of-mouth.

## P10A KEY INSIGHT

> **The most underrated growth lever in DTC is not finding new customers — it's making existing customers buy again.** At your CAC of £15-25, a single-purchase customer generates £14-18 of contribution margin. A two-purchase customer generates £28-36. A three-purchase customer generates £42-54. The entire difference between a failing brand and a thriving one is whether 30% of your customers come back. Every element in this document — from the insert card in the package to the Day 30 NPS email to the Strava challenge to the second-liner cross-sell — exists to move that 30% needle. Build retention into every touchpoint from Day 1, and the business compounds. Ignore it, and you're perpetually buying new customers at a loss.
